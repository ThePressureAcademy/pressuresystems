## The Founding Partner Offer

**LIFTIQ Founding Partner Pilot — 60 to 90 days**

This is a structured pilot, not a free trial. It requires active participation from the partner and produces documented outcomes that both parties benefit from.

### What the Partner Gets

- Full LIFTIQ Phase 1 setup: company, workers, credentials, fatigue records, job types configured to their operation
- Weekly 30-minute review call with the LIFTIQ founder
- Direct founder support channel (not a helpdesk)
- Structured before/after outcome measurement (dispatch time, credential catches, fatigue interventions, swap speed)
- Influence over Phase 1 feature priorities based on their operational feedback
- Founding partner pricing on conversion — discounted lifetime rate, locked before Phase 2 pricing is set
- First right of refusal on design-partner seat for Phase 2 (compliance, credential management, mobile)

### What the Partner Gives

- Agreement to participate in weekly feedback calls
- Agreement to enter real operational data (workers, credentials, fatigue records, jobs) during the pilot period
- Agreement to allow anonymised outcome data to be used in LIFTIQ case study materials (specific numbers, no company name unless they consent)
- Baseline data capture at pilot start (see metrics below)
- Honest post-pilot assessment

### What This Is Not

- Not a finished enterprise product
- Not a payroll, invoicing, or quoting tool
- Not a replacement for existing dispatch processes during the pilot (it runs alongside)
- Not a commitment to build custom features on their behalf

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|

| Monthly pilot fee | Waived |

## Qualification — The Correct First Pilot Customer

Use this checklist before committing to a pilot conversation.

### Must-Have Criteria

| Criterion | What to Look For |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Size** | 15–40 personnel who can be dispatched to crane/lifting jobs |
| **Current dispatch method** | Spreadsheets, whiteboards, paper boards, WhatsApp, or verbal + basic scheduling tools |
| **Visible pain** | At least one of: credential misses or near-misses, fatigue concerns, last-minute swap stress, dispatcher overload, inability to explain allocation decisions |
| **Owner proximity** | Owner or operations manager is still close to daily dispatch; can make decisions without a procurement layer |
| **Feedback commitment** | Willing to participate in weekly calls and enter real data |
| **Outcome sharing** | Willing to allow anonymised outcome data for case study |

### Disqualifying Criteria

| Criterion | Why It Disqualifies |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Already using FCC deeply | Wrong Phase 1 target; complement pitch is Phase 2 |
| Wants payroll or invoicing before fatigue/crew fit | Not the dispatch intelligence buyer |
| Wants LIFTIQ to replace their entire system immediately | Cannot deliver this in Phase 1; sets wrong expectations |
| Demands custom feature ownership | Structurally incompatible with product development model |
| Procurement layer with multiple sign-offs | Too slow; pilot window closes |
| Refuses to share outcome data | Not useful as a design partner; decline politely |
| Company is > 80 personnel at pilot start | Too complex for Phase 1 scope; too many edge cases to support well |

### Diagnostic Questions (Use in Qualification Calls)

1. "Walk me through how you decide who goes on a job when you've got three jobs running and a crew member calls in sick at 6am."
2. "When did you last have a credential issue — someone on-site without the right ticket?"
3. "How do you currently track how many hours your operators have worked this week?"
4. "If I asked you right now which of your operators is most at risk of a fatigue issue this week, how long would it take you to answer?"
5. "What does your dispatcher actually use when making a crew call — is it in their head, a board, a spreadsheet?"
6. "Have you ever had a situation where you wished you could explain to a client or regulator exactly why you allocated someone a particular job?"

**Strong signal answers:**
- "I just know" / "it's in my head" → SmartRank wedge
- "We've had a couple of near-misses" → CredentialGate wedge
- "I don't always know until I add it up manually" → FatigueGuard wedge
- "We had to make a swap last week and it took an hour of phone calls" → SwapEngine wedge
- "If something went wrong, I'd struggle to reconstruct the decision" → AuditIQ wedge

## Baseline Metrics — Capture Before Pilot Starts

Do not start a pilot without a documented baseline. Without it, there is no case study.

### Dispatch Time

Ask the dispatcher to time themselves on three allocation decisions during the week before go-live. Record:
- Time from "job comes in" to "crew confirmed"
- Number of calls/messages made before decision is finalised
- Number of times the first choice fell through

### Credential Exposure

Review their last 60 days of job records (or ask the operator):
- How many times was a credential issue discovered after allocation (on-site or day-of)?
- Were there any near-misses where an expired credential was spotted at the last minute?
- How do they currently check credentials — manually, from memory, from a spreadsheet?

### Fatigue Visibility

Ask:
- Do they currently track hours worked per operator per week?
- Have they ever made an allocation they later wished they hadn't because the worker was clearly tired?
- Has any worker self-reported fatigue and what happened?

### Swap Events

Ask:
- How many last-minute swaps (within 4 hours of job start) happened in the last month?
- How long did each one take to resolve?
- What information did they need and where did they find it?

### Dispatcher Confidence

Before go-live, ask the dispatcher:
- On a scale of 1–10, how confident are you that you're consistently selecting the best available person for each job?
- What information would make you more confident?

## Pilot Structure — Week by Week

### Week 1 — Setup and Baseline
- Configure company, users, roles
- Import all workers with roles and crane classes
- Enter credentials (start with high-risk licences and MSIC cards)
- Enter fatigue records for the past 7 days
- Run SmartRank on one real job to validate setup
- Document baseline metrics

### Weeks 2–4 — Active Dispatch
- All new jobs entered in LIFTIQ before crew is confirmed
- Dispatcher uses SmartRank for every job where multiple options exist
- All allocations logged, including overrides and reasons
- Any credential blocks or fatigue warnings flagged and discussed in weekly call

### Weeks 5–8 — Full Pilot Operation
- Same as Weeks 2–4, but reducing founder involvement in setup
- Focus shifts to: are dispatchers using it without prompting? What friction exists?
- Review pilot dashboard weekly with partner

### Weeks 9–12 (if 90-day pilot)
- Commercial outcomes review
- Compare against baseline
- Case study draft (if they consent)
- Conversion conversation

## Proof Metrics — What Success Looks Like

| Metric | Minimum Signal | Strong Signal |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Dispatch time | No increase from baseline | >20% reduction |
| Credential blocks triggered | At least one caught per month | Credential issue prevented that would have caused a problem |
| Fatigue warnings triggered | At least one warning per week | At least one allocation changed due to fatigue risk |
| SmartRank adoption | >70% of allocations made via LIFTIQ | >90% with dispatcher initiating without prompting |
| Override events | At least some overrides documented with reasons | Overrides decrease as dispatcher gains confidence in rankings |
| Swap resolution time | Comparable to baseline | Measurable reduction |
| Dispatcher confidence score | Reported as same or higher | Reported as clearly higher |

## IF / THEN Qualification Rules

**IF** they ask "can it replace our whole system?" → They are not the first pilot.

**IF** they say "we just need better dispatch visibility and fatigue tracking" → Qualify them further.

**IF** they will not enter real data → The pilot produces no value and no proof.

**IF** they want payroll integration before caring about fatigue → Park them for Phase 3.

**IF** they are already in FCC → Complement pitch only; not Phase 1 target.

**IF** they are on spreadsheets and feel the dispatch pain → They are the ideal Phase 1 partner.

**IF** they will not pay the setup fee → Consider whether the pain is real enough.

**IF** they refuse outcome tracking → Decline the pilot; redirect to general enquiry.

**IF** two candidates are comparable → Prefer the one whose dispatch pain is most clearly around fatigue and credentials, not payroll or invoicing.

## Conversion — After the Pilot

At pilot end, the conversation is not a sales pitch. It is a review:

1. "Here is what LIFTIQ logged during your pilot." (Show the dashboard)
2. "Here is how it compares to your baseline." (Show the numbers)
3. "Here is what would be different in Phase 2." (Compliance, credential management, mobile)
4. "Here is what founding partner pricing looks like if you proceed."

If the pilot produced real credential catches, fatigue interventions, or dispatch time savings — the case makes itself. If it did not produce evidence, understand why before making a sales case.
