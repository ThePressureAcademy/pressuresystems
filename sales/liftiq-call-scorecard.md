# DispatchTalon Call Scorecard

Score each category from 1 to 5.

1 = weak or absent signal.
5 = strong, urgent, and commercially meaningful signal.

## Fit Score

The command centre normalises the 1-to-5 category scores into a 0-to-100 fit score so the bands stay easy to use during a live call.

| Category | Score |
|---|---:|
| Job intake messiness | 1 to 5 |
| Manual allocation pain | 1 to 5 |
| Credential/VOC complexity | 1 to 5 |
| Fatigue/schedule pressure | 1 to 5 |
| Labour-only fit | 1 to 5 |
| Plant + labour complexity | 1 to 5 |
| Asset/plant tracking need | 1 to 5 |
| Crane/transport planning need | 1 to 5 |
| Audit/proof need | 1 to 5 |
| Internal champion strength | 1 to 5 |
| Willingness to test | 1 to 5 |
| Budget readiness | 1 to 5 |
| Support load | 1 to 5 |

## Fit Output

| Score | Recommendation |
|---:|---|
| 0 to 25 | Not ready. Keep in nurture. |
| 26 to 40 | Testing Partner. |
| 41 to 55 | Labour Allocation Pilot or limited Founding Partner. |
| 56 to 70 | Founding Partner Pilot. |
| 71+ | Commercial Pilot or Managed Pilot. |

## Support Score

| Support signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Low support | They can self-test with minimal help. |
| Standard support | Setup call, import help, and light weekly review. |
| Remote Ops Support | Expect configuration help, data cleanup, and screen-share support. |
| On-call support risk | Price as high-touch commercial pilot or do not proceed. |

## Decision Rules

- Do not offer a managed pilot if budget readiness is below 3.
- Do not offer free access if support load is 4 or 5.
- Do not sell plant + labour complexity to labour-only teams.
- Do not offer a commercial pilot without an internal champion.
- If the prospect cannot name one realistic scenario, keep them in nurture.
