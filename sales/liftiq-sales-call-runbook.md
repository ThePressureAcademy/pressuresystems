# LIFTIQ Sales Call Runbook

Internal sales enablement only. Do not publish this file as public site copy.

## Core Positioning

LIFTIQ is a pilot-stage dispatch intelligence layer for labour and lifting operations.

Core line:

> LIFTIQ does not replace dispatch judgement. It structures the decision before dispatch, shows the reasoning, and keeps the audit trail.

Expanded line:

> LIFTIQ helps labour, crane, rigging, plant, transport, and lifting teams structure allocation decisions before dispatch.

## Call Control

1. Identify the operating model before showing screens.
2. Confirm whether they allocate people, plant, or both.
3. Ask current-state questions before explaining features.
4. Show only the shortest proof path that matches the pain.
5. Separate light test access from managed pilot support.
6. End with a dated next step.

## Call Modes

| Mode | Best use | Primary next step |
|---|---|---|
| First conversation | Unknown fit | Discovery or diagnostic |
| Warm referral | Trust already borrowed | Demo or pilot-fit review |
| Labour-only discovery | People allocation only | Labour Allocation Pilot or test portal |
| Plant + labour discovery | People and equipment allocation | Founding Partner or Commercial Pilot |
| Live Teams demo | Clean demo data available | Proposal or test portal |
| Test portal onboarding | Access has been provisioned | Day 3 and Day 14 checkpoints |
| Founding-partner close | Strong early operator | 90-day pilot proposal |
| Commercial pilot close | Higher support and stakeholder load | Commercial pilot proposal |
| Trial check-in | Existing trial user | Keep, support, convert, or stop |
| Trial expiry | End of test period | Decision call |
| Funding/proof conversation | Evidence conversation | Proof pack follow-up |

## Operating Modes

### Labour-only

Use this path for rigging labour suppliers, dogman/rigger crews, crane operators supplied as labour, shutdown crews, civil labour allocators, and workforce coordinators without owned plant.

Show:
- Workers
- Credentials and VOCs
- Availability
- Fatigue
- Schedule
- SmartRank
- Audit
- Metrics

Avoid:
- Asset register unless they ask
- Crane/counterweight detail unless the job brief mentions it
- Fleet-management language

### Plant + Labour

Use this path for crane companies, heavy lift operators, plant hire with operators, transport-supported lifting businesses, and teams managing cranes, low loaders, trailers, and people.

Show:
- Our Business setup
- Company requirement catalogue
- Asset register
- Multiple assets under one class
- Plant number / asset number
- Crane model and travel state
- Counterweight and support transport review
- SmartRank
- Audit
- Metrics

Avoid:
- Maintenance scheduling claims
- Telematics/GPS claims
- Permit approval claims
- Legal road access claims

## Do Not Claim

- Compliance approval
- Permit approval
- Legal road access
- WHS guarantee
- Engineered lift confirmation
- Autonomous dispatch
- Safe to dispatch
- Payroll-ready
- Full ERP replacement
- Fleet Cost & Care replacement today

## Approved Framing

- Dispatcher remains decision-maker
- Before-dispatch clarity
- Explainable recommendations
- Credential and fatigue checks
- Audit trail
- Pilot-stage
- Managed pilot
- Road-access review flags

## Final Call Rule

Do not leave a call with a vague "send me info" ending. Capture one of:
- No fit, parked
- Send context pack
- Book demo
- Open limited test portal
- Build 90-day pilot proposal
- Schedule decision call
