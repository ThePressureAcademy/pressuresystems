# DispatchTalon Objection Handling Bank

![DispatchTalon by Pressure Systems](../assets/logos/dispatchtalon/dispatchtalon-primary-lockup.png)

Use tactical empathy first. Then ask a question. Then state the boundary. Then show proof.

## We only supply labour.

- Empathy: That makes sense. I would not want to show you plant clutter.
- Question: Where do tickets, VOCs, fatigue, availability, and allocation reasoning live now?
- Boundary: DispatchTalon has labour-only mode. Plant features can stay hidden.
- Proof: Labour-only mode, worker import, SmartRank.
- Next step: Labour Allocation Pilot or light test.

## We do not own plant.

- Empathy: Good. Then the plant register may not matter for you.
- Question: Do you still need to track site requirements, operator preferences, or one-off job notes?
- Boundary: DispatchTalon does not need to become a plant system for labour-only teams.
- Proof: Labour-only mode.
- Next step: Keep the demo people-focused.

## We already know who is good.

- Empathy: I believe that.
- Question: Where is that knowledge recorded when someone else has to make the call?
- Boundary: DispatchTalon captures preference signals but the dispatcher still decides.
- Proof: Worker detail, task preferences, SmartRank reasoning.
- Next step: Show the preference trail.

## We have multiple cranes the same size.

- Empathy: That is exactly why class and actual asset should be separate.
- Question: Do you assign the class first and the plant number later, or both at once?
- Boundary: DispatchTalon is not full fleet management, but it can reference plant numbers.
- Proof: Asset Register.
- Next step: Plant + labour pilot fit.

## This sounds like fleet management.

- Empathy: It would be a problem if we pretended it was full fleet management.
- Question: What do you actually need at dispatch: full maintenance status or clear plant reference?
- Boundary: No maintenance, telematics, GPS, depreciation, or asset compliance management in this scope.
- Proof: Asset register and job asset context.
- Next step: Keep the boundary clear.

## This sounds like more admin.

- Empathy: That is the right concern. Bad setup would create admin.
- Question: Which part of job intake is already being typed twice?
- Boundary: The goal is less re-keying and clearer review.
- Proof: Job Brief Import and filtered requirements.
- Next step: Test one scenario.

## We already have a system.

- Empathy: That may be fine. I am not assuming replacement.
- Question: Does your current system explain the allocation decision before it is made?
- Boundary: DispatchTalon can sit as a decision-support layer.
- Proof: SmartRank and audit.
- Next step: Compare one job scenario.

## Can it do payroll?

- Empathy: No. If payroll is the buying reason, DispatchTalon is not the product.
- Question: Is payroll the problem, or is pre-dispatch allocation clarity the problem?
- Boundary: No payroll, timesheets, invoicing, or ERP claim.
- Proof: Guardrails panel.
- Next step: Disqualify or refocus.

## Can it replace FCC?

- Empathy: Not today, and I would not sell it that way.
- Question: What does your current system handle well, and where does pre-dispatch reasoning fall short?
- Boundary: No Fleet Cost & Care replacement claim.
- Proof: Modules page boundary.
- Next step: Position as overlay/pilot.

## What if the recommendation is wrong?

- Empathy: That is why it must be explainable and reviewable.
- Question: How do you currently challenge a bad allocation assumption?
- Boundary: Dispatcher remains decision-maker. Override reasons are captured.
- Proof: SmartRank and override screen.
- Next step: Show audit trail.

## Is this compliant?

- Empathy: I would be careful with that word.
- Question: What review points do you need surfaced before dispatch?
- Boundary: Review required. No compliance approval or WHS guarantee.
- Proof: CredentialGate, FatigueGuard, audit.
- Next step: Use boundary script.

## Can it approve permits?

- Empathy: No. It can surface review flags, not approve permits.
- Question: Where do permit or road-access review notes get captured today?
- Boundary: No permit approval, route approval, or legal road access claim.
- Proof: Crane/counterweight review flags.
- Next step: Keep as review support only.

## We are too busy to test it.

- Empathy: That might be true. The test should be smaller, not bigger.
- Question: What is one repeated job scenario worth testing?
- Boundary: A Testing Partner path can stay limited.
- Proof: Short demo path.
- Next step: Offer one scenario test.

## How much does it cost?

- Empathy: It depends on whether you want light access or managed implementation.
- Question: Are you expecting a login only, or setup, imports, configuration, support, and weekly review?
- Boundary: Pricing is scoped by workflow and support load.
- Proof: Offer ladder.
- Next step: Quote the matching tier.

## We want free access.

- Empathy: A light test can be free or low-cost where strategic. A managed pilot cannot be free.
- Question: What level of support are you expecting from us?
- Boundary: Free access does not include custom implementation.
- Proof: Support scope calculator.
- Next step: Separate Testing Partner from managed pilot.
