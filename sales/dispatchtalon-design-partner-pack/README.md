# DispatchTalon Design Partner Pack

**Status:** Internal operating pack | May 2026
**Owner:** Cody / Pressure Systems
**Purpose:** Identify, qualify, onboard, and manage early DispatchTalon design partners during Step 1.2 of the market entry roadmap.

---

## Positioning

**Core line:** DispatchTalon structures the dispatch decision before work is confirmed.

**Commercial wedge:** A job may need four roles, not four people.

**Boundary:** DispatchTalon is decision support. The dispatcher remains the decision-maker.

**Value pillars used in every conversation:**
1. Job intake clarity
2. Worker / role / credential matching
3. Multi-role allocation coverage
4. SmartRank / CredentialGate / FatigueGuard
5. Publish allocation (manual SMS preview, not direct send)
6. Audit trail
7. CSV office handoff

## Hard rules — what DispatchTalon does not claim or do during a pilot

DispatchTalon during Step 1.2 does **not**:

- Send SMS to workers directly (manual SMS preview only — dispatcher copies and sends)
- Integrate directly with Xero or MYOB (CSV handoff only)
- Calculate payroll or run payroll
- Calculate or issue invoices
- Approve permits, lifts, escorts, road access, vehicle combinations, or any external regulatory matter
- Provide compliance, WHS, NHVR, or legal advice
- Confirm a worker is "safe to dispatch" — the dispatcher confirms
- Provide unlimited support
- Provide custom development inside a standard pilot
- Provide indefinite free access

These constraints are written into every outbound message, the discovery call, the pilot scope, and the agreement.

---

## Banned vocabulary in all materials

Do not use these words in messages, scripts, or scope documents:

- revolutionary
- AI replaces dispatch
- compliant
- safe to dispatch
- Xero integrated
- payroll-ready
- guaranteed
- automatically prevents incidents

Approved language register:
- decision support
- structures the decision
- surfaces credential and fatigue conditions
- review required before dispatch
- explainable recommendation
- dispatcher confirms

## How to use this pack in a typical week

1. **Monday:** Review `internal-lead-scoring-sheet.md`. Pick the 5 highest-scoring leads.
2. **Monday–Tuesday:** Send outbound using messages from `design-partner-outreach-messages.md`.
3. **Mid-week:** Run discovery calls using `design-partner-discovery-call-script.md`.
4. **End of call:** Score lead using `design-partner-qualification-scorecard.md`.
5. **After call:** Send the matching follow-up from `design-partner-follow-up-templates.md`.
6. **When a lead reaches the gate:** Move them through `docs/dispatchtalon-design-partner-gate-criteria.md` and propose the right partner type from `design-partner-pilot-scope.md`.
7. **Once agreed:** Use `design-partner-agreement-outline.md` as the lawyer-review input and `design-partner-success-metrics.md` + `design-partner-feedback-framework.md` from week one.

## Audience

- Crane / rigging / heavy lift operators, 15–40 personnel (best fit)
- Civil and labour-hire businesses with weekly allocation pressure (good fit)
- Operators currently using spreadsheets, OneNote, whiteboards, phone calls, or SMS for allocation
- Operators with an accessible owner or operations manager
- Operators with a real need to record why an allocation decision was made

**Not the audience right now:**
- Operators who want payroll automation today
- Operators who want Xero/MYOB sync today
- Operators who want a full ERP replacement
- Operators with no weekly allocation pressure
- Operators where the decision-maker is unreachable
- Anyone expecting custom development inside a $0–$1,000 testing engagement
