# Design Partner Feedback Framework

**Use:** Set the cadence at kick-off. Drive the weekly review. Drive the exit review at week 12. Feedback is the deliverable Pressure Systems is paying the partner for (with discounted access, structured support, or a future case-study placement). Treat it that way.

**Cadence:**
- Testing Partner — written feedback at end of test window
- Labour Allocation Pilot — bi-weekly 30-minute review
- Founding Partner — weekly 30-minute review
- Commercial Pilot — weekly 60-minute review with action register

---

## The twelve dimensions

The design partner must give feedback on all twelve. Setup must score before the partner is allowed to keep using the system. Adoption is gated by usefulness, not by login count.

| # | Dimension | What we're testing |
|---|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Setup clarity | Did Build My Business setup, role mapping, and credential mapping make sense? |
| 2 | Worker import | Did the CSV import handle their actual worker list? What broke? |
| 3 | Job creation | Is intake fast enough to be used under real time pressure? |
| 4 | Role coverage realism | Does multi-role coverage handle their actual workers and jobs honestly? |
| 5 | SmartRank usefulness | Did the rankings agree with the dispatcher's own intuition? When they didn't, was the explanation good enough to argue with? |
| 6 | CredentialGate / FatigueGuard usefulness | Did the warnings and blocks surface things that mattered, or just create noise? |
| 7 | Publish allocation usefulness | Was the manual SMS preview an upgrade on their current notification flow? |
| 8 | Audit trust | If audited, could they reconstruct who was chosen and why from the audit log alone? |
| 9 | Export usefulness | Did the CSV go straight to admin/accounting without retyping? |
| 10 | Mobile usability | Did anyone try to use it on a phone? What broke? |
| 11 | What they would pay for | Concretely — which features, at what price level? |
| 12 | What blocks adoption | What would stop them paying or continuing? |

## Weekly feedback questions

Use these in every review. Score 1–5 where requested. Capture quotes verbatim where possible — they are the strongest input into the product backlog.

### Operational signal (capture every week)

- How many jobs did you create this week?
- How many allocations did you run?
- How many warnings or blocks surfaced before dispatch?
- How many allocations changed after publish?
- Did anyone log in from a phone?
- Did anyone retype data into another system this week? If yes, what and why?

### Dimension 1 — Setup clarity

- (Kick-off week only) On a 1–5 scale, how clear was Build My Business setup?
- Did you discover anything in week 2 that you wished you'd known at kick-off?

### Dimension 2 — Worker import

- (Once per pilot, after first import) On a 1–5 scale, how well did the import handle your existing worker list?
- What had to be cleaned up manually?
- Anything missing from the worker schema that you actually needed?

### Dimension 3 — Job creation

- On a 1–5 scale, how fast can your team create a job from a rough enquiry?
- Where did the friction land — intake form, requirement catalogue, role mapping?
- What did you give up entering, and what did you work around?

### Dimension 4 — Role coverage realism

- On a 1–5 scale, did the multi-role coverage recommendations match real-world worker capability?
- A job needed N roles — did DispatchTalon suggest a viable smaller crew? Capture an example.
- When it suggested coverage you'd reject, what was missing from the picture?

### Dimension 5 — SmartRank usefulness

- On a 1–5 scale, how often did the top SmartRank suggestion match what you'd have chosen yourself?
- When they disagreed, was the explanation enough to either change your mind or override confidently?
- Any factor you wish was weighted differently?

### Dimension 6 — CredentialGate / FatigueGuard

- On a 1–5 scale, were the credential and fatigue warnings useful or noisy?
- Any false positives — a block that wasn't actually a problem? Capture the case.
- Any false negatives — a real risk we missed? Capture the case.

### Dimension 7 — Publish allocation (manual SMS preview)

- On a 1–5 scale, was the SMS preview easier than your current notification method?
- Did the worker understand the message?
- Did the dispatcher add or remove anything from the preview before sending?

### Dimension 8 — Audit trust

- On a 1–5 scale, if you got audited next month, could you defend last week's allocation decisions from the audit log alone?
- What's missing from the audit record that you'd want there?

### Dimension 9 — Export usefulness

- On a 1–5 scale, did the CSV go into your downstream system cleanly?
- How much manual cleanup did admin still have to do?
- What columns are wrong or missing?

### Dimension 10 — Mobile usability

- Did anyone try to use DispatchTalon from a phone this week?
- What broke?
- What did they fall back to instead?

### Dimension 11 — What they would pay for

- Of everything you used this week, what would you pay for individually?
- What would you pay nothing for?
- Where is the price-point you'd commit to without flinching?

### Dimension 12 — What blocks adoption

- What's the single thing that would stop you continuing past this pilot?
- What would the rest of your team need to see to back continuation?

## Mid-pilot review (week 6) — extended questions

Add to the weekly questions at week 6:

- Compared to your old process, which step is now faster, slower, or unchanged?
- Which DispatchTalon feature have you stopped using? Why?
- Which DispatchTalon feature has surprised you (good or bad)?
- Who in your team is the actual champion? Who's the actual blocker?
- If we stopped the pilot tomorrow, what would you miss most? Least?

## Exit review (week 12) — formal questions

These feed the success-metrics report (Commercial Pilot) or the continuation conversation (Founding / Labour).

- Decision: continue, pause, exit. Why?
- Top three concrete improvements over your previous process?
- Top three things that didn't work or aren't worth paying for?
- What was the dollar value of the change you observed in 90 days? (Working estimate is fine — methodology can be loose.)
- What price would you continue at? On what terms?
- Would you allow an anonymised case study? (Yes / No / Yes with conditions)
- Would you take a reference call from another operator considering DispatchTalon?

## How feedback is captured and used

- All feedback is written down by Cody during or immediately after the review. Voice recordings only with explicit consent and only with written transcripts attached.
- Feedback feeds three places:
  1. The DispatchTalon product backlog
  2. The pilot's running outcome record
  3. The pack itself — if a pattern repeats across two design partners, this framework gets updated
- Verbatim quotes may be reused in DispatchTalon materials only with permission. Anonymised learnings — patterns, not identifying detail — are part of the pilot terms.
- A partner can withhold any specific learning from broader use by saying so during the review. That decision is logged with the feedback.

---

## What this framework is not

- Not a satisfaction survey. NPS-style questions are not in this framework on purpose.
- Not a feature request channel. Requests are recorded and prioritised in the backlog — not promised back.
- Not a substitute for the agreement. Anything that affects scope, billing, or term goes through the agreement, not a review session.

---

*Feedback is the asset the design-partner programme produces. Treat the review with the same discipline as the discovery call: questions before answers, verbatim notes, and follow-up against the previous week's commitments.*
