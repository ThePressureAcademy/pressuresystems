# Design Partner Qualification Scorecard

**Use:** After the discovery call. Score each lead 1–5 across ten dimensions. Total /50.

**Bands:**
- 40–50: Strong fit → propose Founding Partner Pilot or Commercial Pilot
- 30–39: Workable fit → propose Labour Allocation Pilot
- 20–29: Test only → propose Testing Partner (free or low-cost)
- < 20: Not now → polite decline, return to nurture

---

## How to score

Score what you actually heard on the call, not what you hope is true. If you didn't ask the question, score 0 and book a follow-up call to get the answer. Do not guess.

`1 = absent / blocker · 2 = weak · 3 = workable · 4 = strong · 5 = textbook fit`

---

## 1. Allocation pain severity

**Question to test:** "What does a bad allocation week cost you?"

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Specific recent incident — re-allocated mid-week, lost margin, near-miss, customer complaint. They can name it. |
| 4 | Recurring weekly frustration with current process. Specific examples. |
| 3 | They acknowledge friction but it's tolerable. |
| 2 | Vague "yeah it could be better." |
| 1 | "It works fine." Or no allocation pressure. |

## 2. Current manual process weakness

**Question to test:** "Walk me through how a job got allocated last week."

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Multiple disconnected tools (spreadsheet + OneNote + whiteboard + SMS). Reasoning is in someone's head. |
| 4 | One spreadsheet or whiteboard, no audit trail, no role separation. |
| 3 | A scheduling tool exists but doesn't capture decision reasoning. |
| 2 | Existing software covers most of it. |
| 1 | Already on FCC, EQUIPR, or similar — overlap risk. |

## 3. Worker count suitability

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | 15–40 workers — textbook fit |
| 4 | 40–80 workers — Commercial Pilot territory |
| 3 | 80–150 workers — possible but support burden rises |
| 2 | < 10 or > 200 workers — wrong size |
| 1 | < 5 or > 500 workers — not a fit for current scope |

## 4. Job volume suitability

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | 10–40 jobs per week — enough signal, manageable load |
| 4 | 5–10 or 40–80 jobs per week |
| 3 | 2–5 or 80–150 jobs per week |
| 2 | < 2 jobs per week — not enough signal |
| 1 | > 200 jobs per week — current Phase 1 scope too narrow |

## 5. Decision-maker access

**Question to test:** "Who else would need to be on board for you to commit to a pilot?"

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Owner / GM is on the call and has authority |
| 4 | Ops manager on call, owner reachable directly |
| 3 | Ops manager on call, owner approval needed but achievable |
| 2 | Multiple gatekeepers, no clear path |
| 1 | Procurement-led process, board-level approval, no commercial sponsor |

## 6. Feedback willingness

**Question to test:** "Are you willing to do a 30-minute weekly review for 90 days?"

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Explicit yes, names the person, mentions they want input |
| 4 | Yes with caveats on timing |
| 3 | "Probably" |
| 2 | Hesitant, deflects |
| 1 | "Just give us the software" — no feedback appetite |

## 7. Data quality readiness

**Question to test:** "Can you send me your current worker list and last week's job board after this call?"

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Already in spreadsheet form, sends within 24 hours |
| 4 | Exists in messy form, can be tidied in a week |
| 3 | Mostly in people's heads, will need workshop to extract |
| 2 | Scattered across paper and texts |
| 1 | No worker list exists. Credentials uncertain. Roles undefined. |

## 8. Support burden risk

**Question to test:** "How much hand-holding will you need to get a new tool live?"

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Self-driven, will champion internally |
| 4 | Needs onboarding workshop, then self-driven |
| 3 | Needs weekly check-in for 90 days — Founding/Commercial territory |
| 2 | Wants daily support — Managed Support add-on conversation |
| 1 | Expects unlimited support included — decline or restructure |

## 9. Commercial seriousness

**Question to test:** "If this works as described, what's a reasonable monthly price for you?"

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Names a figure ≥ Labour Allocation Pilot tier. Has budget. |
| 4 | Open to paid pilot, needs scope clarity |
| 3 | Wants free trial first, then will consider |
| 2 | "Only if it saves us money" — no budget framing |
| 1 | Expects free forever, or expects payment in exchange for using it |

## 10. Case-study potential

**Question to test:** "If this works, would you be open to us writing it up anonymously?"

| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 5 | Recognisable in the segment, would consider on-record case study |
| 4 | Anonymised case study yes, named no |
| 3 | "Maybe after we see results" |
| 2 | "Probably not" |
| 1 | Strict confidentiality required — no learnings reusable |

## Best-fit profile (for outbound targeting)

- 15–40 personnel
- Crane, rigging, labour-hire, or plant allocation
- Currently using spreadsheets, OneNote, whiteboards, phone calls, or SMS for allocation
- Enough weekly jobs (10+) to test the workflow
- Accessible owner or operations manager
- Willing to give structured feedback weekly

## Poor-fit profile (decline or defer)

- Wants payroll automation in the pilot
- Wants Xero/MYOB sync in the pilot
- Wants a full ERP replacement
- Wants custom development inside a standard pilot
- Expects free unlimited support
- No weekly allocation pressure
- No accessible decision-maker

---

*Scoring is a working judgement, not a yes/no gate. Combined with the gate criteria document, it tells Cody which partner type to propose, not whether to engage.*
