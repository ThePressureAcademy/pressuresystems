# Design Partner Discovery Call Script

**Length:** 30 minutes. 20 minutes of listening, 10 of talking, then ask for the next step.

**Goal of the call:** Decide whether this operator should be offered a Testing, Labour, Founding, or Commercial pilot — or politely declined. Score against `design-partner-qualification-scorecard.md` immediately after.

**Cardinal rule:** Do not pitch features. Map their current process. Their answers tell you which DispatchTalon features matter to them. Then mirror them back.

---

## Opening (2 minutes)

> Thanks for the time. Quick frame: I'm not going to demo today. I want to understand how dispatch and allocation actually run at {{company}} and whether DispatchTalon fits. I'll be honest if it doesn't.
>
> Couple of housekeeping things: this is decision-support, not payroll or compliance software. The dispatcher always confirms allocations. We don't send SMS automatically. We do not approve permits or lifts. If you're looking for any of those today, I'll save us both time and tell you now.
>
> Sound good? Then walk me through your week.

---

## Section 1 — Current dispatch process (4 minutes)

Map their actual workflow. Take notes verbatim where possible.

- "Walk me through how Monday morning starts. Who does what, in what order?"
- "How does a job become a job — who owns it from enquiry to confirmed?"
- "When you say 'allocate,' what does that physically look like — paper, spreadsheet, board, chat?"

## Section 2 — Where job details come from (3 minutes)

- **"Where does the job first land — phone, email, text, OneNote, spreadsheet?"**
- "How often is the job spec complete on first contact?"
- "Who decides what roles are needed for a job? How is that captured?"
- "When the brief is rough, how do you turn it into something dispatchable?"

## Section 3 — How workers are chosen (3 minutes)

- "Talk me through last Monday's allocation. Who went where and why?"
- "Where does the reason for that choice live? In your head, in a chat, in the spreadsheet?"
- **"Where is the reason for the allocation recorded?"**
- "If someone audited you in six months, could you reconstruct who was chosen and why?"

## Section 4 — How credentials are checked (2 minutes)

- "How do you know on Friday afternoon whether the worker you've picked has a current ticket?"
- "Who keeps the credential register? How fresh is it?"
- "What happens when a credential expires the day before a job?"

## Section 5 — How multi-skilled workers are handled (3 minutes) — the commercial wedge

This is the part that sells the product. Do not skip.

- **"Who decides whether one worker can cover multiple roles?"**
- **"How do you currently stop a four-role job becoming a four-person job when it does not need to?"**
- "When two workers can both do role A, but only one can also do role B, how do you split them across two jobs?"
- "How often do you over-allocate because no-one mapped role-coverage properly?"

## Section 6 — How many roles per job (2 minutes)

- "On a typical job, how many roles are on the manifest — operator, dogman, rigger, supervisor, others?"
- "Are role lists written down anywhere or do they sit with the supervisor?"
- "How many of those roles can be filled by the same person, in practice?"

## Section 7 — How plant/assets are tracked (2 minutes)

- "Plant: where does the asset list live? Spreadsheet, FCC, paper?"
- "When you allocate a crane to a job, where is that recorded?"
- "Are crane and operator allocated together, or separately?"
- "If you're labour-only, skip this — I just want to confirm scope."

## Section 8 — How allocation changes are handled (2 minutes)

- "Worker calls in sick Friday at 4pm. What happens? Who chooses the replacement? Where is the swap recorded?"
- "When the customer changes the start time on the morning of, how does that ripple through?"

## Section 9 — How workers are notified (2 minutes)

- "When an allocation is final, how does the worker actually find out where they're going?"
- "SMS, phone, WhatsApp, app, board?"
- "How do you know the message landed? How do you know they read it?"
- "When you change an allocation last-minute, how does the new instruction land?"

## Section 10 — How office/admin gets the data (2 minutes)

- "Once a job is done, what does the office need from you?"
- "How do they get it today — spreadsheet, email, paper run-sheet, screenshot?"
- "How much retyping happens between you and them?"
- "When payroll runs, where does the time data come from?"

## Section 11 — What they use now (2 minutes)

- "What software is involved in any of this today? FCC, EQUIPR, Excel, OneNote, paper, WhatsApp, Procore — anything I've missed?"
- "What's working? What's the thing that everyone complains about?"
- **"What happens when the person who knows the system is away?"**

## Section 12 — What would make a pilot successful (3 minutes)

- "If we ran a 90-day pilot and it worked, what would change in your week?"
- "What would make you say 'this is worth paying for'?"
- "What would make you say 'don't bother continuing'?"
- "Who else would need to be on board for you to commit to a pilot?"
- "Are you willing to do a 30-minute weekly review for 90 days?"

---

## Closing the call (2 minutes)

If the call is a clear no, say so politely:

> Honest read: I don't think DispatchTalon's where you need to be right now. {{reason — too small / wants payroll automation / already on FCC and happy / no allocation pressure}}. If that changes, message me.

If the call is a maybe, set the gate:

> A few things I'd want to see before we structure a pilot: {{specifics — worker list / job sample / weekly review commitment / decision-maker on the next call}}. Send those across this week, and I'll come back with a partner type proposal that fits.

If the call is a strong yes, propose the next step:

> Based on what you've described, the shape that fits is {{Testing / Labour / Founding / Commercial}}. Let me put together a scope and pricing summary tonight. We'll review tomorrow, and if it lands well, kick-off is the week of {{date}}.

---

## What not to do on the call

- Do not open a screen-share unless they ask. The call is about their process, not the product.
- Do not promise SMS sending, Xero integration, payroll, invoicing, custom features, or compliance approval.
- Do not give a price on the call unless you can also give the scope.
- Do not name a competitor's customer or operator without permission.
- Do not commit to a kick-off date until the worker list and job sample arrive.

---

*Script is a working frame, not a recital. Adjust phrasing to the operator. The numbered questions are the ones that should not be skipped — they are what the qualification scorecard depends on.*
