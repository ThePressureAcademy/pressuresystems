## 1. Purpose

A practical, founder-led sales sheet for triaging the **first five real DispatchTalon targets**.

This is not the full pipeline tool (`internal-lead-scoring-sheet.md` is that). This is the lightweight, "open it on a Sunday night and decide what to do Monday morning" sheet. It exists to answer one question for each of five named leads:

- Is this person worth calling this week, parking, or declining — and if calling, what tier do I offer?

It is deliberately small. Five rows, one screen, no CRM.

When the first five have either moved into a pilot or been parked, retire this sheet and graduate to the full lead scoring sheet.

---

## 2. How to use

**Once, at setup:**

1. Put a real name + company in each of the five rows. If you have fewer than five named candidates, leave the remaining rows blank — do not pad with cold names.
2. Score from public knowledge first (see Section 4). Fill what you can. Use `TBC` for unknowns.
3. Compute the **Total score /25** = pain + decision-maker + feedback + support-burden (reverse-scored) + commercial seriousness.
4. Set **Fit category** from the bands in Section 5.
5. Set **Recommended tier** using Section 6.
6. Set **Next action** from the standard list in Section 8.

**Weekly Monday morning, 15 minutes:**

- Walk the five rows top to bottom.
- For each: did the next action happen? Is there new information that changes the score?
- Re-rank, re-set the next action with a date, and commit to no more than two outbound moves this week per lead.
- When a lead enters Gate 2 (discovery done) they move out of this sheet and into `internal-lead-scoring-sheet.md`.

**Honest hygiene:**

- No padding. If you cannot name five real candidates, the sheet has four rows.
- No fantasy scoring. If a score would make the lead look better than the evidence, leave the score lower.
- No prices in any outbound message until the scope is agreed (`design-partner-follow-up-templates.md` covers the sequence).

## 4. Scoring guide

Score five dimensions from **1 to 5**. Total is out of 25.

### 4.1 Pain severity /5

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Mild curiosity, no obvious dispatch pain |
| 2 | Occasional manual friction |
| 3 | Clear dispatch friction but not urgent |
| 4 | Regular pain with workers / jobs / credentials / schedule |
| 5 | Urgent pain; current system clearly failing or wasting time and money |

### 4.2 Decision-maker access /5

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Unknown contact, no authority |
| 2 | Low influence, would need referral |
| 3 | Can influence but not approve |
| 4 | Direct operations manager / owner access likely |
| 5 | Owner / director / operations decision-maker directly accessible |

### 4.3 Feedback willingness /5

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Unlikely to give structured feedback |
| 2 | Casual feedback only |
| 3 | Willing after demo |
| 4 | Likely to participate in weekly / fortnightly feedback |
| 5 | Highly engaged; design-partner / reference candidate |

### 4.4 Support burden risk /5 (reverse-scored)

This dimension is reverse-scored — **higher number = better fit**. A chaotic, high-burden lead scores 1; a clean, realistic lead scores 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Very high support burden, chaotic, expects everything free |
| 2 | High support burden |
| 3 | Manageable support burden |
| 4 | Low support burden |
| 5 | Clear, organised, realistic expectations |

### 4.5 Commercial seriousness /5

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Tyre kicker |
| 2 | Interested but no budget / action |
| 3 | Possible paid pilot later |
| 4 | Likely to pay if fit is proven |
| 5 | Strong commercial intent; can approve a pilot |

### 4.6 Total score /25

`pain + decision-maker access + feedback willingness + support-burden (reverse-scored) + commercial seriousness`

### 4.7 How to score from public knowledge (before any call)

Use what's visible without speaking to them yet:

- **Website:** services listed, fleet/plant photos, locations, "about" depth
- **LinkedIn:** company size band, named ops manager / dispatcher, recent hiring of coordinators
- **Known relationship:** prior contact, referral source, mutual connections
- **Company size signals:** number of crews, depots, visible vehicles
- **Visible fleet / plant:** crane count, truck count, equipment categories
- **Role of contact:** owner / GM / ops manager / dispatcher / coordinator / admin
- **Job-volume clues:** project case studies, recurring client patterns, gallery posts
- **Current dispatch clues:** if they advertise "call us 24/7" or list dispatcher phone numbers, that's a paper/phone process

From public-only signals you can usually score:
- decision-maker access (role of contact)
- support burden risk (organised website / clear comms = higher)
- commercial seriousness (size + project visibility)

You usually **cannot** score from public signals:
- pain severity (need conversation)
- feedback willingness (need conversation)

Leave those at `TBC` or a conservative 2–3 until first contact.

### 4.8 How to score after a discovery call

Update the row within 30 minutes of the call ending:

- **Current dispatch method:** what they actually use today (verbatim — "OneNote", "the whiteboard", "a WhatsApp group with the foremen")
- **Pain severity:** based on what the dispatcher described, not on what the salesperson hopes
- **Support burden risk:** clean data? known worker list? credentials in one place? — higher = better
- **Recommended tier:** apply Section 6 to the new total
- **Next action:** set from the standard list in Section 8 with a specific date

## 5. Fit categories

| Total /25 | Category | Meaning |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| 22–25 | **Priority Design Partner** | Likely Founding Partner or Commercial Pilot candidate |
| 18–21 | **Strong Pilot Candidate** | Likely Labour Allocation Pilot, or Founding Partner if complexity supports it |
| 14–17 | **Testing Partner / Nurture** | Good for light validation, not yet a high-touch pilot |
| 10–13 | **Low Priority** | Only follow up if strategically useful (referral path, sector reach) |
| < 10 | **Decline / park** | Do not spend meaningful time yet |

## 6. Recommended tier logic

Pricing is **internal working model only**. Do not write these numbers into outbound messages, the public site, or anything a partner sees before scope is agreed (see `design-partner-follow-up-templates.md` for the right sequence).

### Testing Partner — $0–$1,000 setup / 14–30 days / limited test access

Use when:
- Score 14–17
- Useful feedback potential but weak budget
- Small operator (under ~15 workers or under ~3 jobs/week)
- Strategically useful relationship (referral path, sector intelligence)
- Not enough data to justify a paid pilot yet

### Labour Allocation Pilot — $2,500 setup + $1,500/month / 90 days / $7,000 total

Use when:
- Labour-only or mostly labour
- Up to ~50 workers
- Minimal plant complexity
- Score 18–21
- Enough weekly jobs (≥ 5/week) to actually exercise the workflow

### Founding Partner Pilot — $5,000 setup + $2,500/month / 90 days / $12,500 total

Use when:
- Serious operator
- Labour + plant, or complex labour-only allocation
- Up to ~100 workers
- Up to ~25 assets
- Strong feedback potential (likely weekly reviews held)
- Possible reference / anonymised case study at exit
- Score 22+

### Commercial Pilot — $10,000 setup + $5,000/month / 90 days / $25,000 total

Use when:
- Formal evaluation expected
- Multiple allocators / dispatchers
- Management-level approval
- Plant + labour complexity
- Up to ~250 workers / ~75 assets
- Formal reporting required
- High commercial seriousness (5/5)

### Managed / On-call Support — $8,000–$10,000/month add-on

Use when:
- High ongoing support expectation
- Multi-branch operation
- Complex worker / asset data import
- Weekly optimisation or active hand-holding requested
- Partner wants Cody / Pressure Systems actively involved

This is an **add-on**, not a standalone tier. It attaches to a Founding or Commercial Pilot.

### Tier override notes

- A score of 22+ with **single-system-mandate**, **wants-payroll**, or **wants-xero** as a risk flag goes back to Testing Partner or polite decline. Do not push someone into a paid pilot they will be unhappy with.
- A score of 18–21 with **plant + labour complexity** can be promoted to Founding Partner — explain why in the **Reason for tier** column.
- A score below 14 with a strategic relationship reason (former employer, sector champion) can be parked at Testing Partner — explain why in **Owner notes**.

## 7. Questions to ask before assigning a tier

Cover these in the discovery call (`design-partner-discovery-call-script.md` is the full script). Do not assign a tier until at least eight of the ten have an answer.

1. Where does the job first land — phone, email, text, OneNote, spreadsheet?
2. How many workers or operators are you allocating weekly?
3. How many jobs are you coordinating weekly?
4. Do you allocate labour only, plant + labour, or both?
5. How do you know who has the right ticket or credential for a given job?
6. Who decides whether one worker can cover more than one role?
7. How do you stop a four-role job becoming a four-person job when it does not need to?
8. How do you notify workers after allocation?
9. Where is the reason for the allocation recorded?
10. What would make a 90-day pilot worth continuing?

The **commercial wedge** is questions 6 and 7. If the lead has no clean answer to either, DispatchTalon's role-coverage logic is a real lever for them. If they have a clean answer, the wedge is weaker — re-check whether DispatchTalon is genuinely the right product.

## 8. Next action options

Use **only** these values in the `Next action` column so the sheet sorts cleanly:

| Value | Use when |
|-------|----------|
| `Research more` | Not enough public signal yet to even open contact |
| `Send warm DM` | Existing LinkedIn / referral connection — `design-partner-outreach-messages.md` template |
| `Send context-first email` | Cold but real — use the operator-aware email template, not a generic pitch |
| `Book discovery call` | Reply received, ready for the 30-minute call |
| `Invite to Testing Partner` | Discovery done, score 14–17, scope is a 14–30 day light test |
| `Offer Labour Allocation Pilot` | Discovery done, score 18–21, labour-only fit |
| `Offer Founding Partner Pilot` | Discovery done, score 22+, labour + plant or strong feedback |
| `Offer Commercial Pilot` | Discovery done, score 22+, formal evaluation, multiple allocators |
| `Defer / nurture` | Score 10–13, or risk flag blocks immediate action — six-month timer |
| `Decline politely` | Score < 10, hard misfit, or risk flag (`wants-payroll`, `single-system-mandate`, `competitor-overlap` — see `internal-lead-scoring-sheet.md`) — use template B4 from `design-partner-follow-up-templates.md` |

Every `Next action` row should also have a date attached in the **Follow-up date** column.

## 9. Weekly review cadence

**Sunday night (or Monday morning), 15 minutes:**

1. Open this sheet alongside calendar.
2. For each of the five rows:
   - Did last week's next action happen? Yes → update **Contact status**, **Last contact date**, **Owner notes**. No → why not.
   - Did anything change the score? Update.
   - Reset **Next action** + **Follow-up date** for this week.
3. Confirm capacity: at most **two active outbound moves per week per lead**. Founder-led sales is not volume sales.
4. If a lead has been at the same score with no movement for 30 days, move them to **Defer / nurture** with a six-month timer and re-open the slot.
5. When a lead clears Gate 2 (`docs/dispatchtalon-design-partner-gate-criteria.md`), graduate the row into `internal-lead-scoring-sheet.md` and free the slot.

**Monthly:**

- Are all five slots filled with real names? If not, the constraint is sourcing — block an hour to refresh.
- Are the same names sitting at score < 14 for two months in a row? They are not the first five. Decline / park and replace.

**At "first five complete":**

- Retire this sheet (move to archive, do not edit further).
- All active pipeline lives in `internal-lead-scoring-sheet.md` from that point.
- Capture lessons from the first five in `design-partner-feedback-framework.md` so the next cohort scores better.

## What this sheet is not

- Not a CRM — once you have more than five live leads, move to `internal-lead-scoring-sheet.md`
- Not a marketing list — outbound is opt-in by sector context, not blast
- Not a contract or pricing commitment — pricing is internal working model only until scope is agreed and the agreement (`design-partner-agreement-outline.md`) is lawyer-reviewed and signed
- Not a substitute for honest scoring — if a lead scores poorly, they score poorly

---

*Founder-led sales gets fewer at-bats than corporate sales, so each at-bat has to be a considered swing. The point of this sheet is to make sure the first five swings are the right ones.*
