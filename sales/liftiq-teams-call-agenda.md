| Time | Segment | Purpose |
|---:|---|---|
| 0:00 to 0:03 | Opening and boundary | Set practical tone. No magic AI. No autonomous dispatch. |
| 0:03 to 0:10 | Current workflow | Understand people, plant, systems, and pain. |
| 0:10 to 0:16 | Mode split | Determine labour-only or plant + labour path. |
| 0:16 to 0:24 | Focused proof | Show public page, screenshot, or short portal path. |
| 0:24 to 0:28 | Fit and support scope | Light test or managed pilot. |
| 0:28 to 0:30 | Next step | Date, owner, action. |

## Screen Share Decision Tree

| Situation | Use |
|---|---|
| Internet and demo tenant are smooth | Live portal |
| Portal login or data is not ready | Screenshots |
| Prospect is time-poor | 3-minute walkthrough |
| Prospect is operations-heavy | Live portal + audit + metrics |
| Prospect is owner/commercial | Public page + offer path + proof assets |
| Prospect is safety/compliance-focused | Credential/fatigue/audit/review boundary |
| Prospect is crane-operations-focused | Job Brief Import + asset register + crane/counterweight review |

## First Words

> Appreciate the time. I will keep this practical. I am not here to pitch some magic AI dispatch system. DispatchTalon is being built because dispatch in labour and lifting has too many moving parts sitting in people's heads: job notes, crew, credentials, fatigue, schedule conflicts, plant references, counterweight, transport, and the decision trail. I want to understand how you handle that now, then show you where DispatchTalon may or may not fit.

## Last Words

> Based on what we discussed, the next step is either a light test, a managed pilot scope, or no fit for now. I do not want to leave this vague.
