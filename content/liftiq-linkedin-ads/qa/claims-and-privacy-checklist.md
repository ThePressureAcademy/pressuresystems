# DispatchTalon LinkedIn Ads Claims and Privacy Checklist

## Privacy Checklist

- [x] No passwords visible.
- [x] No JWTs visible.
- [x] No Fly secrets visible.
- [x] No tenant admin emails visible.
- [x] No private worker names visible.
- [x] No private phone numbers visible.
- [x] No real customer job notes visible.
- [x] No real company operational data visible.
- [x] Synthetic/demo preview panels are labelled as `Pilot workflow preview`.
- [x] Public screenshot source is a public DispatchTalon page screenshot.

## Do Not Claim in Ads

These phrases must not appear as positive ad claims:

- compliance approval
- permit approval
- safe to dispatch
- legal to travel
- guaranteed compliance
- autonomous dispatch
- replaces dispatcher
- replaces Fleet Cost & Care
- permit approved
- engineered lift confirmed
- prevents incidents

## Approved Framing Used

- review before dispatch
- decision-support
- dispatcher remains the decision-maker
- audit trail
- review flags
- road-access review
- credential/fatigue checks
- explainable recommendations
- pilot-stage

## Ad-Level Claim Notes

| Ad | Claim boundary |
|---|---|
| Job Intake | The ad says reviewed requirements before allocation. It does not say the system creates dispatch decisions automatically. |
| SmartRank | The ad says visible reasoning and keeps dispatcher control. It does not say SmartRank allocates autonomously. |
| Operating Modes | The ad says the portal can be configured by operating model. It does not imply HR, payroll, ERP, or fleet-management completeness. |
| Crane Transport Review | The ad says review flags and planning context. It explicitly avoids permit, road-access, or lift-engineering approval. |
