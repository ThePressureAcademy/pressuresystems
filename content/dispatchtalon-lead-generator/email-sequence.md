# Email Sequence

Internal sales asset. Four context-first emails for operations managers and named-contact owners who do not live on LinkedIn.

UTM convention for every link in this sequence:

```
?utm_source=email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=drd-v1&utm_content=<email_slug>
```

Rules across all four:

- Founder voice. First-person. Cody-signed.
- No public pricing.
- No claim outside the boundary list.
- One CTA per email.
- 7 day gap between sends. Pause the sequence the moment the lead replies.
- Max four emails per lead. No multi-month drip.
- Subject lines should pass the operator-on-a-Friday-afternoon test (short, specific, no marketing tone).

- A different way to count dispatch

Body:

```

Following up on the dispatch readiness check from last week.

A job may need four roles, not four people.

Truck Driver x2. Dogman x1. Rigger x1.

## Email 3. Audit and office handoff

Slug: `e3-audit`
Send day: Day 14
Goal: Shift from front-of-house dispatch pressure to the back-of-house cost of missing structure.

Subject line options:

- The cost of a six week old allocation
- Why office staff retype dispatch data
- The audit trail your dispatcher cannot write twice

Body:

```
Hi [First name],

Two more layers the diagnostic checks for, which usually surprise owners more than dispatchers.

2. Office handoff. Office and admin staff should never re-key what dispatch already entered. When they do, you are paying twice for the same data, and the second copy will drift from the first.

Neither of those is a dispatch pain on the day. Both are a cost the business absorbs quietly across the year.

## Pause and exit rules

- The moment a recipient replies, pause the sequence and continue the conversation by hand.
- If a recipient completes the diagnostic, do not send the remaining emails. Move them into the follow-up framework instead.
- If a recipient unsubscribes or says "not interested," remove them from all future sends.
- If a recipient has not engaged after Email 4, mark them in the lead sheet as "email cold" and do not re-attempt for at least 90 days.

---

## Tracking

- Every link in this sequence must carry the UTM convention above.
- Lead sheet captures: send date per email, opens or replies if measurable, diagnostic completion if linked back, final disposition.
- Compare reply rate by subject line variant. After 20 sends per variant, prefer the higher reply rate for the next batch.
