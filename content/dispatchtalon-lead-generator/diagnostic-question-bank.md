# Dispatch Readiness Diagnostic - Question Bank

**Internal sales asset. Companion to `dispatch-readiness-diagnostic.html`.**

This is the authoritative source for the 15 questions, their option sets, and the score per option. If you change a question or option here, mirror it into the HTML before the next campaign sends.

Scoring scale:

- **0** - completely manual / not visible
- **1** - informal / inconsistent
- **2** - partially structured
- **3** - mostly structured
- **4** - structured, reviewed, and repeatable

Max score: **60** (15 questions × 4).

---

## Category 1 - Job Intake Clarity

### Q1. Where does the job first land?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| Phone call / memory | 0 |
| SMS / WhatsApp | 1 |
| Email | 2 |
| OneNote / spreadsheet | 3 |
| Structured job intake | 4 |

**Rationale:** Intake quality is the upstream constraint on every downstream decision. Picking *where the job first lands* (not where it eventually ends up) reveals the real exposure.

---

### Q2. Are job requirements captured before dispatch?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| Rarely | 0 |
| Sometimes | 1 |
| Usually | 2 |
| Always | 3 |
| Structured and reviewed | 4 |

**Rationale:** Captured-but-not-reviewed is still exposure. The fourth point is the difference between a habit and a process.

---

### Q3. Are role counts recorded?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| No - we work it out at dispatch | 0 |
| Sometimes, in a message | 1 |
| In notes / spreadsheet | 2 |
| In a structured field | 3 |
| Structured and visible to ops | 4 |

**Rationale:** Role counts are the seed of the multi-role wedge. Without them, "four roles, not four people" can't be evaluated.

## Category 2 - Worker / Role / Credential Visibility

### Q4. Can you quickly see who can perform each role?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| Only in the dispatcher's head | 0 |
| Scattered in messages / notes | 1 |
| Partly in a spreadsheet | 2 |
| In a maintained roster | 3 |
| Structured by role per worker | 4 |

**Rationale:** Surfaces the single-point-of-failure that owners often don't see until the dispatcher is sick.

---

### Q5. Can you quickly see who has each ticket / credential?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| Not without asking | 0 |
| In a folder somewhere | 1 |
| Tracked in a spreadsheet | 2 |
| Tracked with expiries | 3 |
| Structured per worker with expiries | 4 |

**Rationale:** "Tracked" without expiries is a slow leak. The fourth point separates a register from a working system.

---

### Q6. Are VOCs, site requirements, or specialist credentials visible before allocation?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| Never | 0 |
| Rarely | 1 |
| Sometimes for major sites | 2 |
| Mostly - we check before publish | 3 |
| Always - structured into the decision | 4 |

**Rationale:** Site- and customer-specific requirements are the single biggest source of "we sent the wrong person" incidents.

## Category 3 - Multi-Role Allocation Coverage

### Q7. How do you decide if one worker can cover multiple roles?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| Gut feel in the moment | 0 |
| Phone the worker | 1 |
| Notes / informal pattern | 2 |
| Documented per worker | 3 |
| Suggested by system, confirmed by ops | 4 |

**Rationale:** The "suggested + confirmed" pattern is exactly the DispatchTalon shape. Self-scoring against it surfaces interest.

---

### Q8. Do you compare conservative headcount against realistic role coverage?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| We just count bodies | 0 |
| We try, but not consistently | 1 |
| Sometimes, for known crew | 2 |
| Most jobs | 3 |
| Structured - every multi-role job | 4 |

**Rationale:** Drives the wedge home in operator language.

---

### Q9. Can your current system show that a four-role job may only need two workers?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| No - we always send four | 0 |
| Only if the dispatcher remembers | 1 |
| Sometimes, manually | 2 |
| Usually, manually | 3 |
| Yes - suggested with warnings | 4 |

**Rationale:** This is the wedge question. Most operators will score 0–2. The gap is the conversation.

## Category 4 - Dispatch Control / Publish Process

### Q10. Is worker notification separate from internal allocation selection?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| Same step - we just message them | 0 |
| Loosely separated | 1 |
| Separated in process, not in tool | 2 |
| Separated, with a check before notify | 3 |
| Structured publish step after review | 4 |

**Rationale:** Most operators conflate selection and notification. Separating them is a behaviour change DispatchTalon supports.

---

### Q11. Can you preview what the worker receives before publishing?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| No | 0 |
| We re-read the message | 1 |
| We preview informally | 2 |
| We preview formally most of the time | 3 |
| Always preview - structured field | 4 |

**Rationale:** Previewable publish is the safety net before the SMS path triggers.

---

### Q12. Are changes / overrides recorded?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| No | 0 |
| Sometimes, in messages | 1 |
| In notes | 2 |
| In a tracked log | 3 |
| Structured override + reason captured | 4 |

**Rationale:** Override-with-reason is the bridge to audit. Without it, the audit log is decorative.

### Q14. Can office / admin export job & allocation data without retyping?

| Mixed | 2 |

**Rationale:** CSV office handoff is an approved capability claim. This question probes the cost of *not* having it.

---

### Q15. Can management review warnings, overrides, and allocation history later?

| Option | Score |
|---|---|
| No record exists | 0 |
| Only in scattered messages | 1 |
| Partial - notes / spreadsheet | 2 |
| Yes - most decisions | 3 |
| Yes - full reviewable history | 4 |

**Rationale:** Closes the audit category. Sets up the ops-manager and owner conversation about visibility.

## Edit rules

- Do not add a question without a category fit and a clear DispatchTalon mapping.
- Do not introduce 5+ option scales. Keep all questions at 5 options scored 0–4 so the total max stays at 60.
- Do not soften the wedge questions (Q7–Q9). They are the conversion pivot.
- Every option label must be language an operator would actually use, not consultant-speak.
