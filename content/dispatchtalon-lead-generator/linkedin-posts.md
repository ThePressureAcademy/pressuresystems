```

Truck Driver x2. Dogman x1. Rigger x1.
```

## Post 3 - The trail, not the note

**Slug:** `missingtrail`

```
If your dispatch decision lives in one person's head, the risk is not the note.

It is the missing trail.

The dispatcher knows why they picked the crew they picked. That knowledge doesn't leave the building with them at 5pm - it never enters the building. It stays in their head.

Six weeks later, when management asks why Worker A got allocated and Worker B didn't, the honest answer is usually: "I trusted the dispatcher's call."

That's fine when the call is good. It's expensive when it isn't.

DispatchTalon doesn't replace the dispatcher. It captures the trail behind the call.

3-minute check on whether your trail is structured: [diagnostic link]
```

**Why it works:** Inverts the obvious framing. The risk operators worry about (the note) is not the real risk.

## Carousel adaptation notes

Any of posts 1–5 can be adapted into a LinkedIn carousel using this slide map:

| Slide | Content |
|---|---|
| 1 | Hook (the post's opening line, big type) |
| 2 | The setup (1–2 sentences of context) |
| 3 | The problem in operator language |
| 4 | The "missing layer" framing |
| 5 | One concrete example (e.g., the truck-driver-and-dogman case) |
| 6 | What DispatchTalon does inside the claim boundary |
| 7 | What it does NOT do (preserves boundary, signals credibility) |
| 8 | CTA: 3-minute check + diagnostic link |

Carousel visual rules:

- Charcoal background, cream text, rust accent line on each slide.
- One sentence per slide max (except slides 5 and 7).
- No stock photography.
- Final slide carries the diagnostic URL clearly and a small `DispatchTalon · by Pressure Systems` mark.

## Reply playbook

When an operator comments **TALON**:

1. Like the comment.
2. Reply publicly: "Sending - also dropping a question or two so I can give you a useful read."
3. DM with the diagnostic link.
4. After they complete: read their summary, score them against the lead funnel, follow the `follow-up-framework.md` script for their band.

When an operator comments with **disagreement or pushback**:

1. Don't argue.
2. Reply: "Fair - what's the part of dispatch you'd say works fine in your operation?" Use their answer to refine the question bank.

## What NOT to post on LinkedIn

- Pricing.
- Compliance / safety / engineering claims.
- "We're hiring" cross-posts mixed in with diagnostic posts.
- Anything that uses LIFTIQ as the active brand.
- Generic SaaS founder posts ("Lessons from year one…").
- AI hype framing. DispatchTalon doesn't sell AI. It sells structured decision-support.
