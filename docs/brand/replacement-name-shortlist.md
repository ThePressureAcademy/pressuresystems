# Replacement Name Shortlist

Capture date: 2026-05-14

Status: screening candidates only. This is not legal advice. No candidate is legally cleared.

Migration update: DispatchTalon has been selected as the preferred working replacement name for the controlled brand migration. Attorney clearance remains recommended before further brand investment.

## Naming Decision Frame

Current working name LIFTIQ is high-risk for continued public scale because user-supplied IP Australia screenshots show existing/protected LIFTIQ marks in software/SaaS-relevant Classes 9 and 42.

Replacement name requirements:

- Work under Pressure Systems as the parent brand.
- Cover labour-only allocation and plant + labour allocation.
- Avoid Lift, IQ, compliance, safe, permit, legal, engineered, autonomous, payroll, ERP, and Fleet Cost & Care replacement implications.
- Sound industrial and operational, not generic SaaS.
- Be distinctive enough to justify attorney screening.

Recommended product architecture:

> Pressure Systems [ProductName]

Descriptor:

> Dispatch intelligence for labour and lifting operations.

## Top 10 For Attorney And Domain Screening

### 1. DispatchTalon

Positioning line:

> DispatchTalon is Pressure Systems' dispatch intelligence platform for labour and lifting operations.

Why screen it:

- "Dispatch" immediately identifies the category-entry point.
- "Talon" creates a strong industrial mark without using Lift/IQ.
- Works for labour-only and plant + labour modes.
- Good migration from LIFTIQ because the descriptor carries the product story.

Risks to screen:

- Exact and similar "Talon" marks in software, logistics, operations, field service, aviation, industrial inspection, and workforce tools.
- Visual/phonetic variants such as Dispatch Talon, Talon Dispatch, TalonOps, TalonGrid.

### 2. CrewTalon

Positioning line:

> CrewTalon helps teams structure labour allocation decisions before dispatch.

Why screen it:

- Strongest labour-only fit.
- Still serious enough for industrial crews, rigging crews, shutdown teams, and operators.
- Avoids crane-only perception.

Risks to screen:

- "Crew" is crowded in workforce, scheduling, field operations, and construction software.
- May need public descriptor to keep plant + labour mode obvious.

### 3. FieldKeel

Positioning line:

> FieldKeel gives labour and lifting teams a stable decision layer before dispatch.

Why screen it:

- Broad enough for labour, plant, transport, crane review, audit, and future expansion.
- More distinctive than FieldGrid / WorkCore / OpsCore.
- "Keel" suggests control and stability without claiming safety or compliance.

Risks to screen:

- "Field" is crowded in field-service software.
- "Keel" may need brand explanation in sales material.

### 4. DispatchKeel

Why screen it:

- Strong category clarity.
- "Keel" creates distinctiveness and decision-support meaning.
- Slightly less aggressive than DispatchTalon.

Risk:

- Less instantly memorable than Talon.

### 5. PlantTalon

Why screen it:

- Strong match for asset register, plant numbers, equipment classes, crane/transport review.
- Good for plant + labour operators.

Risk:

- Too plant-focused if labour-only is a major growth path.

### 6. YardTalon

Why screen it:

- Strong operational / industrial feel.
- Could work for equipment, transport, crews, and dispatch boards.

Risk:

- "Yard" may imply depot/yard-only operations.

### 7. CrewKeel

Why screen it:

- Strong labour-only mode fit.
- Distinctive enough for screening.

Risk:

- Less dispatch-forward.

### 8. RigTalon

Why screen it:

- Strong rigging/lifting credibility.
- Good for crane/rigging target market.

Risk:

- Too narrow if labour-only/civil/plant allocation expands.

### 9. SiteKeel

Why screen it:

- Broad, serious, field-oriented.
- Works for jobs, sites, crews, plant, and audit.

Risk:

- Less obviously a dispatch platform.

### 10. LoadTalon

Why screen it:

- Strong lifting/load association without using "Lift".
- Industrial and memorable.

Risk:

- May skew toward lift/load planning rather than dispatch intelligence.

## Top 3 Recommendation

### Recommended Primary: DispatchTalon

Use if the business wants the name to explain the category quickly.

Best use:

> Pressure Systems DispatchTalon

Support descriptor:

> Dispatch intelligence for labour and lifting operations.

Why it wins:

- Clear enough for first-touch industrial buyers.
- Distinctive enough to justify screening.
- Does not imply compliance, permit approval, engineering approval, or autonomy.
- Can hold both labour-only and plant + labour workflows.

### Recommended Labour-Forward Alternative: CrewTalon

Use if labour-only allocation becomes the commercial wedge.

Why:

- Strongest direct fit for worker allocation, credentials, fatigue, scheduling, SmartRank, and audit.
- Good for labour hire, rigging crews, shutdown crews, dogmen/riggers, crane operators supplied as labour, and civil labour allocation.

Concern:

- Needs copy support for plant + labour capability.

### Recommended Broad Enterprise Alternative: FieldKeel

Use if the long-term platform needs to feel broader than dispatch.

Why:

- Field/labour/plant/site capable.
- Less direct but potentially more scalable.
- Better than generic names like FieldGrid or WorkCore.

Concern:

- Requires more explanation than DispatchTalon.

## Names Rejected Or Deprioritised

| Name type | Examples | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Lift/IQ names | LiftOps, LiftGrid, LiftReady, any IQ suffix | Too close to current LIFTIQ risk zone. |
| Generic grids | DispatchGrid, CrewGrid, FieldGrid, JobGrid | Easy to understand but weak distinctiveness and crowded. |
| Readiness names | JobReady, WorkReady, OpsReady | Potentially implies readiness/safety assurance and weak as a mark. |
| Compliance/safety names | SafeDispatch, ComplianceLift, PermitReady | Conflicts with product boundary and claim restrictions. |
| Crane-only names | CraneGrid, CraneTalon | Too narrow for labour-only mode. |
| Asset-only names | AssetGrid, AssetTalon | May imply fleet/asset management rather than dispatch intelligence. |

## Copy Test For The Top 3

### DispatchTalon

Short:

> DispatchTalon structures labour and lifting allocation decisions before dispatch.

Boundary:

> It does not replace the dispatcher, approve permits, engineer lifts, or guarantee compliance.

### CrewTalon

Short:

> CrewTalon helps labour teams match workers to job requirements with visible reasoning and audit.

Boundary:

> It supports allocation review; it does not make the dispatch decision.

### FieldKeel

Short:

> FieldKeel gives labour and lifting teams a structured decision layer for jobs, workers, plant context, and audit.

Boundary:

> It keeps review before dispatch and avoids compliance or engineering approval claims.
