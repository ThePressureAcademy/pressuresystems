# Trade Mark Attorney Brief - LIFTIQ Replacement Name Review

Capture date: 2026-05-14

Prepared for: trade mark attorney review.

Important: This brief is not legal advice. It is a business and product-context pack to support professional clearance.

Migration update: DispatchTalon is now the selected working replacement name for implementation. This brief still requires attorney review and does not claim legal clearance.

## 1. Background

The current product working name is LIFTIQ.

The business has received screenshots from IP Australia showing existing registered/protected "LIFTIQ" marks in Classes 9 and 42. Because the product is software/SaaS, these classes are directly relevant.

The business wants to assess whether to replace LIFTIQ before external pilot expansion and public brand investment.

## 5. Current Name Risk Question

Current mark:

> LIFTIQ

Concern:

- User-supplied IP Australia screenshots show existing/protected LIFTIQ marks in Classes 9 and 42.
- IP Australia class guidance lists Class 9 as including downloadable software / information technology equipment.
- IP Australia class guidance lists Class 42 as including computer programming and non-downloadable software services.
- The current product is a software/SaaS product.
- The word "Lift" also has direct semantic relevance to the crane/lifting market.
- The "IQ" suffix is common in software/intelligence naming and close to "Lift IQ".

Request:

- Please assess whether LIFTIQ should be abandoned, limited, filed, opposed, or replaced.
- Please advise whether continued public use creates unacceptable risk.

## 6. Preferred Naming Direction

The business wants a replacement name that is:

- Industrial.
- Serious.
- Operational.
- Clear.
- Not hype-driven.
- Suitable for labour + plant allocation.
- Strong under Pressure Systems.
- Scalable into managed-pilot and enterprise future.
- Distinctive enough for formal clearance.
- Not too crane-only.
- Not too labour-only.
- Not close to LIFTIQ.
- Not using "IQ".

- LiftReady.
- SafeDispatch.
- ComplianceOps.

- LegalRoad.
- EngineeredLift.
- WHSReady.

- Generic marks such as DispatchGrid, CrewGrid, JobGrid, FieldGrid unless there is a strong filing path.

## 11. Suggested Classes To Consider

Please determine final class strategy. Business expects the key classes may include:

- Class 9: downloadable software / mobile apps / software.
- Class 42: SaaS / hosted software / non-downloadable dispatch intelligence platform.
- Class 35: operations administration / workforce allocation support if appropriate.
- Class 39: transport/logistics review support if appropriate.
- Class 41: training/onboarding if productized training assets are marketed under the mark.

Please advise whether Class 39 creates avoidable expansion risk given the product does not perform route optimisation, permit approval, or legal road-access approval.

## 13. Known Competitor And Adjacent Category Context

Known/adjacent brands found in public category review:

- Fleet Cost & Care: crane, rigging, and equipment fleet/operations software.
- A1A Software / 3D Lift Plan: lift planning and heavy construction software.
- Assignar: construction field/crew/equipment operations software.
- Other generic/crowded term families: Dispatch, Crew, Ops, Field, Job, Grid, Ready, Core, Lift, IQ.

Business wants to avoid any claim that the product replaces Fleet Cost & Care or performs lift engineering.
