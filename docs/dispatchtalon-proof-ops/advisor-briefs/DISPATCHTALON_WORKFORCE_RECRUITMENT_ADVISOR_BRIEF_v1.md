# DispatchTalon Workforce / Recruitment Advisor Brief v1

Status: Founder-review-ready draft
Audience: Recruitment, labour-hire, workforce allocation, construction business development, and people-placement advisors
External use: Not approved until founder review

## 1. Purpose

This brief asks workforce and recruitment advisors to test whether DispatchTalon's matching logic makes sense beyond crane dispatch.

The review question is direct: does the idea of matching people, assets, credentials, site requirements, and job context feel commercially useful to businesses that allocate labour?

## 2. Matching Logic Explanation

DispatchTalon is a pilot-stage dispatch intelligence layer.

It helps operational teams review who appears suitable for a job before dispatch is confirmed.

The core idea is:

> Suitability before availability.

Availability matters, but it is only the first filter. A worker may be free and still need review because of role fit, credential state, site requirements, workload context, asset pairing, notes, or the job's operating conditions.

## 3. Recruitment Analogy

Recruitment is not just finding a person who is available.

Good recruitment matches:

- role requirements
- candidate capability
- credential or ticket status
- experience
- client expectations
- timing
- cultural and operational fit
- risk that needs human review

DispatchTalon applies a similar principle to operational allocation.

Instead of matching only a candidate to a role, it supports review across people, assets, credentials, workload, site context, and job requirements before the job goes out.

## 4. Current Product Surfaces

Current pilot-stage surfaces include:

- SmartRank decision-support logic
- worker and asset visibility
- worker credential register
- custom credential types
- Daily Site Log
- worker sign in and sign out
- historical onsite lookup
- printable daily report
- Assisted Source Upload for pilot setup review
- Our Business setup
- diagnostic flow
- personalisation profile
- advisor demo tenants with sample data

Assisted Source Upload is designed to reduce setup friction. It lets a pilot user submit existing business records for assisted review, but it does not automatically import or update live records.

## 5. Workforce / Labour-Hire Applicability

DispatchTalon may be useful anywhere work allocation depends on more than availability.

Potentially relevant environments include:

- labour hire
- construction workforce allocation
- field operations
- plant and equipment support teams
- multi-site contractor coordination
- high-credential or induction-heavy teams

This does not mean the current pilot is already a broad recruitment platform. The question for advisors is whether the matching logic is strong enough to adapt later.

## 6. Commercial Language Questions

Please challenge the language.

We need to know:

- whether "suitability before availability" is clear
- whether "dispatch intelligence layer" sounds too technical
- whether "matching logic" is easier for workforce buyers
- whether "reasoning layer before the job goes out" makes sense
- whether buyers would understand why messy setup data matters
- whether this sounds useful without sounding like compliance approval

## 7. Advisor Questions

1. Does the matching logic make sense from a recruitment perspective?
2. Is it clear that availability is only the first filter?
3. Would construction workforce, labour-hire, or business-development buyers understand the value?
4. What would make this easier to sell in plain English?
5. What objections would recruitment or labour-hire buyers raise?
6. What language feels too technical?
7. What language feels commercially useful?
8. Could this be adapted beyond crane dispatch without losing focus?
9. What would make a business owner trust the review logic?
10. What proof should be collected from pilot users before making broader claims?

## What DispatchTalon Is Not

DispatchTalon is not:

- autonomous dispatch
- a recruitment agency platform
- payroll approval
- compliance approval
- safety approval
- legal attendance certification
- an automatic document-import system
- a guarantee of worker suitability

The responsible operator remains the final decision-maker.

## Controlled Access Option

Advisor review can be done through a clean demo tenant with fake workers, fake assets, sample credentials, Daily Site Log examples, and setup workflows.

The demo environment must not include real pilot-user data, private uploaded documents, real credentials, advisor private information, or internal admin material.

## Boundaries And Claim Safety

Use DispatchTalon as pilot-stage, human-confirmed decision support.

Do not present it as automatic approval, legal advice, compliance approval, payroll approval, or guaranteed business performance.

This brief is for founder-reviewed advisor challenge. It is not public collateral.
