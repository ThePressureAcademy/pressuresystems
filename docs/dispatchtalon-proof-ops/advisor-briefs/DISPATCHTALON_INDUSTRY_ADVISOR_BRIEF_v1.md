# DispatchTalon Industry Advisor Brief v1

Status: Founder-review-ready draft
Audience: Senior crane, rigging, plant, labour, and field-operations advisors
External use: Not approved until founder review

## 1. Purpose

This brief asks experienced industry operators to challenge whether DispatchTalon solves a real operational gap before wider exposure.

DispatchTalon is being shaped with pilot feedback. The review request is practical: does this help the people who have to allocate workers, assets, credentials, and site requirements under pressure?

## 2. Short Product Explanation

DispatchTalon is a pilot-stage dispatch intelligence layer for crane, rigging, labour, plant, transport, construction, and field operations teams.

It helps an allocator review the job before work is confirmed by bringing worker, asset, credential, site, and operational context into one decision-support workflow.

Core wedge:

> Suitability before availability.

The commercial question is not only "who is free?" It is "who appears suitable, credentialed, prepared, and reviewable for this job?"

## 3. Why This Matters

Many field-operations businesses still depend on whiteboards, spreadsheets, phone calls, messages, folders, and one experienced person who remembers how everything fits together.

That can work until:

- a key allocator is unavailable
- a site needs a specific credential or induction
- a worker or asset is available but not the right fit
- expired or missing records are discovered late
- a client asks for a clearer operational record
- the business grows faster than the informal system

DispatchTalon is designed to support the reasoning layer before the job goes out.

## 4. Current Pilot Capabilities

Current pilot-stage capabilities include:

- SmartRank decision-support logic
- worker and asset visibility
- worker credential register
- custom credential types
- Daily Site Log
- worker sign in and sign out
- historical onsite lookup
- printable daily report
- Assisted Source Upload for pilot setup review
- Our Business setup
- diagnostic flow
- personalisation profile
- advisor demo tenants with sample data

## 5. What DispatchTalon Is Not

DispatchTalon is not:

- autonomous dispatch
- compliance approval
- safety approval
- legal attendance certification
- payroll approval
- lift engineering approval
- a full ERP replacement
- an automatic document-import system

The dispatcher or responsible operator remains the decision-maker.

## 6. What We Need From Industry Advisors

Please challenge:

- whether "suitability before availability" is the right frame
- whether the workflow reflects real crane, rigging, plant, and labour operations
- whether the Daily Site Log feels useful as an operational record
- whether the credential and site-requirement language is safe and realistic
- whether the product is too narrow, too broad, or commercially clear enough
- what evidence would be needed before wider industry exposure
- what claims should be avoided completely

## 7. Questions For Review

1. Does this solve a real operational gap?
2. Is "suitability before availability" a useful way to explain the problem?
3. What would make operators trust the SmartRank or review logic?
4. Which workflows feel immediately useful, and which feel optional?
5. What should DispatchTalon avoid claiming?
6. What would need to be true before industry buyers took this seriously?
7. Does the Daily Site Log feel like a practical site diary, or does it need different language?
8. What would make this easier to explain to an owner, allocator, or operations manager?
9. What would make you hesitate to recommend it?
10. What proof should be collected during the next 30 days?

## 8. Controlled Access Option

Advisor review can be done through a clean demo tenant with sample workers, assets, credentials, Daily Site Log records, and assisted setup examples.

The demo environment must not include real pilot-user data, private uploaded documents, real credentials, or internal admin material.

## Boundaries And Claim Safety

Use DispatchTalon as pilot-stage decision support.

Do not present it as certification, compliance approval, safety approval, legal advice, payroll approval, autonomous dispatch, or a guaranteed commercial outcome.

This brief is for founder-reviewed advisor challenge. It is not public collateral.
