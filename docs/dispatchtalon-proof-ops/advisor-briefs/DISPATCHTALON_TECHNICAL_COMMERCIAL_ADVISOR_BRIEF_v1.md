# DispatchTalon Technical / Commercial Advisor Brief v1

Status: Founder-review-ready draft
Audience: Software engineering, commercialisation, governance, security, ISO, grant, and R&D advisors
External use: Not approved until founder review and specialist review where required

## 1. Purpose

This brief asks technical and commercial advisors to challenge DispatchTalon's product direction, architecture posture, evidence base, and claim boundaries before broader commercial use.

The goal is not to make DispatchTalon sound bigger than it is. The goal is to test whether the current pilot product is disciplined enough to become a credible operational platform if development remains focused.

## 2. Product Category

DispatchTalon is a pilot-stage dispatch intelligence layer.

It sits between rough job demand and confirmed dispatch, helping operators review worker, asset, credential, site, workload, and job context before a human confirms the allocation.

Core wedge:

> Suitability before availability.

Commercial shorthand:

> The reasoning layer before the job goes out.

DispatchTalon is not positioned as a full ERP, field-service suite, fleet telematics platform, accounting system, payroll system, or compliance approval product.

## 3. Current Architecture / Product Surface

Current live or pilot-stage surfaces include:

- SmartRank decision-support logic
- worker and asset visibility
- worker credential register
- custom credential types
- Daily Site Log
- worker sign in and sign out
- historical onsite lookup
- printable daily report
- Assisted Source Upload for pilot setup review
- Our Business setup
- diagnostic flow
- personalisation profile
- advisor demo tenants with sample data

Current product boundaries:

- no autonomous dispatch
- no AI automatic import into live records
- no automatic extraction from uploaded documents
- no compliance approval
- no legal attendance certification
- no payroll approval
- no safety approval
- no enterprise custom-role access model in production

## 4. Source-Verified Competitor Landscape Summary

Internal source verification confirms there are serious adjacent and incumbent systems across:

- crane and heavy-equipment operations software
- rental and plant-hire ERP systems
- construction resource planning systems
- field-service and trade workflow systems
- fleet, telematics, and equipment-intelligence systems
- daily-reporting and site-diary systems

DispatchTalon should not claim category exclusivity. The safer distinction is focus: a narrow pilot-stage suitability reasoning and operational-record layer for businesses that need reviewable dispatch decisions before they are ready for, or willing to start with, a heavier ERP-style system.

Detailed competitor names should stay in controlled internal appendices unless founder approval and source review confirm they are needed for the audience.

## 5. Strategic Wedge

DispatchTalon's strongest current wedge is not "better scheduling."

The stronger frame is:

- availability is only the first filter
- suitability includes credentials, role fit, asset fit, site requirements, workload, notes, and review status
- the dispatcher confirms the decision
- the system keeps reasoning visible before and after the job
- assisted setup reduces the friction caused by messy business records

This makes DispatchTalon more useful as decision support than as a generic dispatch board.

## 6. Technical / Commercial Risks To Challenge

Please challenge:

- whether the data model is heading in the right direction
- where tenant isolation, privacy, and access-control boundaries must tighten before more users
- whether SmartRank is explainable enough for operator trust
- what evidence is needed before commercial claims are made
- which workflows should remain manual or human-confirmed
- what should not be built yet
- whether advisor demo tenants are sufficient for early review
- whether future extraction, integrations, or role controls should wait until pilot demand is proven
- what documentation, audit, retention, and security artefacts should exist before larger customers
