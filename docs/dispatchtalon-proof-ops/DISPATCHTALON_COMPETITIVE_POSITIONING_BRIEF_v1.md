## Executive Verdict

DispatchTalon should be positioned as a dispatch intelligence layer for crane, rigging, labour, plant, transport, and heavy operations businesses.

The strongest current wedge is:

> Suitability before availability.

Most operators already know who is available. The harder question is whether the person, asset, credential state, site requirement, workload context, and decision trail make that allocation reviewable before the job goes out.

Source verification confirms DispatchTalon should not claim category exclusivity across dispatch, credential, daily-log, fleet, rental, or field-operations software. The safer and stronger claim is that DispatchTalon is focused on a narrower pilot-stage reasoning layer before dispatch: matching job requirements against people, assets, credentials, site context, and review status in a way a dispatcher can confirm.

## What DispatchTalon Is

DispatchTalon is an Australian-built dispatch intelligence layer for operational teams that allocate workers, assets, credentials, and job requirements under time pressure.

It supports:

- worker and asset visibility
- credential and site requirement review
- SmartRank decision support
- Daily Site Log and onsite history
- Assisted Source Upload for pilot setup review
- custom credential types
- printable operational reports
- reviewable allocation reasoning

DispatchTalon helps structure the decision before work is confirmed.

## What DispatchTalon Is Not

DispatchTalon is not:

- an ERP
- autonomous dispatch
- payroll software
- a compliance approval system
- lift engineering software
- a fleet maintenance platform
- a generic field service app
- a legal attendance record system
- an accounting or invoicing platform

The dispatcher remains responsible for the final operational decision.

## Category: Dispatch Intelligence Layer

The category language should stay narrow and useful:

Dispatch Intelligence Layer means a decision-support layer that sits between rough job demand and confirmed dispatch.

It helps answer:

- What does this job require?
- Who appears suitable, not just available?
- Which credentials, site requirements, or asset constraints need review?
- What reasoning should be visible before dispatch?
- What record exists after the decision?

This category is more accurate than ERP, scheduling, field service management, or workforce management.

## Wedge: Suitability Before Availability

Availability is the starting filter.

DispatchTalon should lead with suitability because industrial allocation often depends on:

- role fit
- credential state
- site induction or client requirement
- asset match
- workload and fatigue context
- schedule conflict
- review notes
- dispatcher judgement

The commercial line:

> The reasoning layer before the job goes out.

- AroFlo and Simpro are serious ANZ field-service/trade workflow comparisons.

The strongest safe DispatchTalon distinction is not category exclusivity. It is focus: pilot-stage dispatch decision support, human-confirmed SmartRank suitability reasoning, setup relief, Daily Site Log, custom credential types, and reviewable operational records for businesses that are not ready to implement or do not want to start with a full ERP-style system.

## Real Competitors: Spreadsheets, WhatsApp, Whiteboards, Memory

The strongest immediate competitor is not always software.

DispatchTalon must beat:

- spreadsheet rosters
- WhatsApp allocation threads
- SMS and phone calls
- whiteboards
- OneNote pages
- memory held by one experienced allocator
- disconnected credential folders
- old job sheets and printed reports

The sale is not just "better software." It is reduced operational dependence on memory and scattered information.

## DispatchTalon Differentiation

DispatchTalon should differentiate around:

- suitability before availability
- reviewable SmartRank reasoning
- worker, asset, credential, and site requirement visibility in one dispatch context
- Daily Site Log as an operational record, not payroll
- Assisted Source Upload as onboarding relief, not automatic import
- custom credential types for business and regional reality
- pilot-stage support for companies that do not have clean data yet
- human-confirmed decision support
