## Verification Standard

This matrix verifies competitor positioning from official product pages, official help/documentation pages, official announcements, credible industry publications, and selected customer/case-study pages.

Source confidence ratings:

| Rating | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| high | Official source or strong industry source directly supports the claim. |
| medium | Source supports the broad category, but details should be checked before external use. |
| low | Weak support or indirect inference. |
| needs_review | Do not use externally until verified. |

## Cross-Source Conclusions

The safest shared conclusions are:

- There are serious existing systems across crane dispatch, rental ERP, field service, construction resource planning, daily reporting, fleet management, telematics, and credential visibility.
- DispatchTalon should not claim to be the first, only, or sole product in dispatch, credential tracking, daily logs, fleet visibility, or construction resource planning.
- The more defensible positioning is narrower: pilot-stage dispatch decision support focused on suitability before availability, human-confirmed SmartRank reasoning, setup relief, Daily Site Log, custom credential types, and reviewable operational records.
- The strongest immediate competitor for small and mid-sized operators may still be memory, spreadsheets, WhatsApp, whiteboards, and scattered files, but that is an internal sales insight, not a public market claim without evidence.

## Claims To Remove Or Soften Before External Use

- "No competitor offers ranked suitability reasoning." Replace with: "In the public sources reviewed, competitors more often emphasise dispatch boards, rental operations, field reporting, fleet/asset management, or broad resource planning than a narrow pilot-stage suitability reasoning layer."
- "No competitor has this." Replace with: "DispatchTalon is being shaped around a specific workflow gap."
- "Visual Dispatch has no credential capability." Replace with: "Visual Dispatch public pages and industry coverage reference skills, qualifications, and certifications, so differentiation should not rely on absence of credential tracking."
- "Raken does not compete with Daily Site Log." Replace with: "Raken is a strong daily-reporting comparison; DispatchTalon must differentiate through allocation context and worker/asset/credential suitability."
- "Australian-built means Australian Made." Do not use unless formally verified and approved.
- "Grant/R&D eligible." Do not use; refer only to themes for specialist review.
- "Sovereign hosting." Do not use unless infrastructure, data residency, and contractual posture are verified.

## External-Use Gate

Before public use:

1. Re-check every source URL.
2. Remove detailed competitor comparisons unless legal/commercial review approves them.
3. Keep competitor references high-level and factual.
4. Avoid negative claims about competitor gaps unless the source clearly supports the statement.
5. Use the advisor-ready draft for review, not the raw competitive scan.
