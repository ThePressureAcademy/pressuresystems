Status: Advisor-ready draft
Use: External advisor review after founder approval
Boundary: Do not include passwords, private pilot records, or internal admin material

## Why This Exists

DispatchTalon has moved from concept into an active pilot product. The next step is not more feature volume. The next step is stronger proof.

We are asking selected advisors to challenge whether DispatchTalon solves a real operational gap for businesses that allocate people, assets, credentials, and job requirements under pressure.

## What DispatchTalon Is

DispatchTalon is an Australian-built dispatch intelligence layer for crane, rigging, labour, plant, transport, construction, and field operations teams.

It helps structure the decision before the job goes out.

The core wedge is:

> Suitability before availability.

Availability matters, but it is not enough. Dispatch decisions also depend on credentials, role fit, site requirements, asset context, workload, notes, and human review.

## What It Is Not

DispatchTalon is not:

- autonomous dispatch
- payroll software
- an ERP replacement
- compliance approval
- lift engineering approval
- legal attendance certification
- fleet maintenance software
- a generic field service app

The product supports operational decisions. The dispatcher still confirms the decision.

## What Has Been Built So Far

Current pilot capabilities include:

- worker and asset records
- custom credential types
- worker credential register
- SmartRank decision support
- Daily Site Log
- worker sign in / sign out
- historical onsite lookup
- printable daily report
- Assisted Source Upload for pilot setup review
- Our Business setup
- diagnostic and personalisation flows
- advisor demo tenants with fake data

## What Pilot Users Are Testing

Pilot users and advisors are reviewing whether DispatchTalon can help answer:

- Who is suitable for this job?
- Which worker, credential, asset, or site requirement needs review?
- Who was onsite today?
- Can a daily site record be printed?
- Can existing business records be uploaded for assisted setup review?
- Does the product reduce dependence on memory, whiteboards, and scattered records?

## The Strategic Question

The strategic question is not "can DispatchTalon become a large system?"

The useful question is:

> Does DispatchTalon create enough clarity before dispatch that an operator would trust it, use it, and pay for it?

## What We Need Advisors To Challenge

Please challenge:

- whether "Dispatch Intelligence Layer" is the right category
- whether "suitability before availability" is the right wedge
- whether the product feels commercially credible
- whether the workflow is simple enough for busy operators
- whether the claim boundaries are clear
- whether buyers would understand this without a long explanation
- what should be removed, delayed, or not built yet

Use these terms:

- decision support
- dispatcher confirms
- human confirmed
- reviewable reasoning
- operational record
- assisted setup
- pilot-stage

Do not describe DispatchTalon as:

- autonomous dispatch
- compliance approved
- safety approved
- certified
- payroll approval
- guaranteed ROI
- AI automatically importing records
- replacing the dispatcher

Advisor feedback should strengthen the product's evidence base without pushing it into claims the product has not earned.
