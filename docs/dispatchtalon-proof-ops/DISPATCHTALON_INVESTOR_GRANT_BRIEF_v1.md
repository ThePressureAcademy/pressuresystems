# DispatchTalon Investor / Grant Brief v1

Status: Internal working draft
Use: Investor, grant, R&D, and commercialisation preparation
Boundary: This is not funding advice, legal advice, tax advice, or a grant eligibility claim

## Problem

Heavy operations businesses often allocate workers, assets, credentials, site requirements, and job details using a mixture of memory, spreadsheets, phone calls, WhatsApp, whiteboards, and disconnected systems.

This creates operational pressure:

- the right person may not have the right credential
- available workers may not be suitable
- job requirements may be remembered but not structured
- site logs and daily records may be hard to retrieve later
- business-critical knowledge may sit in one person's head
- setup friction can block software adoption before value is tested

## Market Context

DispatchTalon is focused on crane, rigging, labour, plant, transport, construction, and field operations teams that need practical dispatch decision support before deeper ERP or integration work is justified.

The first market is not broad enterprise transformation. It is controlled pilot evidence from operators who feel dispatch pressure now.

## Competitor Gap

Current research suggests the market includes dispatch boards, field service tools, rental ERPs, fleet systems, construction platforms, telematics tools, and daily reporting apps.

DispatchTalon's possible gap is narrower:

> A reviewable dispatch intelligence layer that reasons across worker suitability, credentials, role needs, asset context, site requirements, and daily operational records before dispatch is confirmed.

This gap requires further market and source verification before external claims are made.

## Product Wedge

The product wedge is:

> Suitability before availability.

Availability is visible in many systems. Suitability is harder because it depends on context, credentials, workload, assets, site requirements, and human judgement.

## Current Product State

Current pilot capabilities include:

- Assisted Source Upload
- Daily Site Log
- worker sign in / sign out
- historical onsite lookup
- printable daily report
- custom credential types
- worker credential register
- SmartRank decision-support logic
- Our Business setup
- personalisation profile

This is an active pilot product. It is not yet enterprise-proven.

## 90-Day Milestones

1. Run controlled advisor reviews across industry, recruitment, software, governance, and grant/R&D lenses.
2. Activate a small number of pilot users with clean demo and live workflows.
3. Collect evidence on site logs, sign in/out, source upload, SmartRank, and credential-type usage.
4. Document pilot objections and trust blockers.
5. Produce a claim-safe commercial proof pack.
6. Decide whether paid pilot conversion is justified.
7. Identify which integrations or automations should not be built yet.

## Risks

- Overstating product maturity.
- Selling ERP replacement when the product is a dispatch intelligence layer.
- Confusing decision support with compliance approval.
- Building features before pilot evidence justifies them.
- Taking grant or R&D assumptions as fact before specialist review.
- Collecting weak feedback without measurable usage.
- Allowing demo data or private pilot data to leak into external proof.

## Evidence Required Before Claims

Before external claims, collect:

- verified pilot usage
- advisor feedback
- operator quotes with permission
- before/after workflow examples
- screenshots with fake or approved data only
- paid pilot intent or signed agreement
- competitor source verification
- specialist advice for grant or R&D language

Do not claim eligibility, funding likelihood, ROI, compliance effect, or market uniqueness without evidence.
