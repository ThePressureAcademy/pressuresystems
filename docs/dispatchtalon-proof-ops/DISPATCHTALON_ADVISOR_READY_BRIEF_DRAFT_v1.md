## Why This Exists

DispatchTalon is being tested as a pilot-stage decision-support layer for businesses that allocate people, assets, credentials, and job requirements under pressure.

The immediate question is not whether it can become a large platform. The useful question is whether it creates enough clarity before dispatch that an operator would trust it, use it, and help shape it.

## What DispatchTalon Is

DispatchTalon is a pilot-stage dispatch intelligence layer for crane, rigging, labour, plant, transport, construction, and field operations teams.

It helps structure allocation decisions before work is confirmed by showing worker, asset, credential, site, and operational context in one reviewable workflow.

Core positioning:

> Suitability before availability.

Availability matters, but it is not enough. A suitable allocation may also depend on role fit, credential state, site requirements, asset suitability, workload context, notes, and dispatcher judgement.

## What DispatchTalon Is Not

DispatchTalon is not:

- autonomous dispatch
- payroll approval
- compliance approval
- legal attendance certification
- lift engineering approval
- a full ERP replacement
- an accounting or invoicing platform
- a fleet telematics platform
- an automatic document-import system

The dispatcher remains responsible for the final operational decision.

## Market Gap

Many operational teams still rely on whiteboards, spreadsheets, WhatsApp threads, phone calls, folders, memory, and one experienced allocator who knows where everything is.

That can work while the business is small and stable. It becomes fragile when:

- the allocator is unavailable
- credentials expire or are hard to find
- a client asks for a clearer record
- a site needs a specific mix of workers and assets
- a business grows faster than its informal process
- a pilot team needs records before building a full ERP

DispatchTalon is being shaped around that gap.

## Competitor Landscape In Plain English

Source verification shows that the market already contains serious systems:

- crane and heavy-equipment operations platforms such as EQUIPR, Visual Dispatch / RapidWorks, Assignar / CraneOps, and Fleet Cost & Care
- rental ERP and plant-hire systems such as Wynne RentalMan and Point of Rental / Syrinx
- construction resource and scheduling platforms such as HCSS, Trimble B2W, and Procore
- fleet, telematics, and asset platforms such as EquipmentShare T3, Tenna, and Trackunit
- field service and trade systems such as AroFlo and Simpro
- daily-reporting and field-record systems such as Raken and Procore Site Diary

DispatchTalon should not claim these systems do not exist or that it owns the category. The safer positioning is that DispatchTalon is being built as a focused, pilot-stage decision-support and operational-record layer for teams that need suitability reasoning before dispatch.

## Current Pilot Capabilities

Current live/pilot capabilities include:

- SmartRank decision-support logic
- worker and asset visibility
- worker credential register
- custom credential types
- Daily Site Log
- worker sign in and sign out
- historical onsite lookup
- printable daily report
- Assisted Source Upload for pilot setup review
- diagnostic flow
- personalisation profile
- advisor demo tenants with sample data

No uploaded document is automatically parsed into live records. No dispatch is approved automatically. No credential status is compliance approval.

## Why Australian-Built Matters, Carefully Worded

Australian-built can be used as an origin and trust signal where it is true and relevant.

It may matter because local operators use local language around licences, inductions, site requirements, VOCs, labour, plant, crane work, and operating conditions.

Do not describe DispatchTalon as Australian Made, government endorsed, CICA endorsed, sovereign-hosted, or grant/R&D eligible unless those claims are formally verified and approved.

## What We Need Advisors To Challenge

Please challenge:

- whether "Dispatch Intelligence Layer" is clear or too abstract
- whether "suitability before availability" lands with operators
- whether the workflow feels useful before a business buys a full ERP
- whether the product should start with crane workflows or broaden into workforce allocation
- whether the Daily Site Log is framed clearly as an operational record
- whether the Assisted Source Upload promise reduces onboarding friction without creating false confidence
- whether the product feels too technical, too narrow, or too broad
- what evidence would be needed before recommending it

## Feedback Questions

1. Does this solve a real operational gap?
2. Is availability clearly positioned as the starting filter, not the final answer?
3. Would an owner, allocator, or operations manager understand the value quickly?
4. Which competitor category would a buyer compare it against at the start of a sales conversation?
5. What would make the SmartRank or suitability logic trustworthy?
6. What evidence should be collected during the next 30 days?
7. What language feels too technical, risky, or inflated?
8. What should be removed, delayed, or not built yet?
9. Could this extend beyond crane dispatch into labour hire, construction recruitment, plant hire, or field operations?
10. What would stop you from recommending this to an operator?

## Review Boundary
