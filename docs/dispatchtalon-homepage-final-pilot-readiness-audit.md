# DispatchTalon Homepage Final Pilot Readiness Audit

Date: 2026-05-16

## 1. Executive Verdict

Ready for human release review. The public DispatchTalon landing page now explains the current pilot-stage product as dispatch intelligence for labour and lifting operations, with multi-role allocation coverage positioned as the main commercial differentiator after PR #44.

The page keeps the route at `/liftiq/`, preserves DispatchTalon branding, avoids public pricing, and keeps claim boundaries visible around payroll, accounting, compliance, permits, legal road access, lift engineering, autonomous dispatch, and automatic SMS sending.

## 2. Commercial Positioning Score

Score: 9/10

The homepage now moves beyond generic scheduling language. The line "A job may need four roles, not four people" makes the commercial usefulness clear for crane, rigging, labour, plant, and transport operators with multi-skilled workers.

## 3. Buyer Clarity Score

Score: 8/10

The page answers what DispatchTalon is, who it is for, how labour-only and plant + labour modes differ, how setup starts, and what happens after allocation. The buyer can see the path from rough intake to role coverage, SmartRank, publish, audit, and export.

## 4. Retention Architecture Score

Score: 8/10

The workflow is now repeatable: Build My Business, worker setup, job intake, role coverage, SmartRank, publish allocation, audit, and Export Centre. This supports pilot retention because the page sells the recurring operating loop, not just a one-time feature set.

## 5. Trust-Boundary Score

Score: 9/10

The landing page makes the dispatcher control point explicit and avoids overclaims. It states that DispatchTalon does not approve role combinations, replace the dispatcher, send SMS automatically, calculate payroll, post to accounting systems, approve permits, engineer lifts, or guarantee compliance.

## 6. Homepage Density Score

Score: 8/10

The homepage remains detailed but uses short cards, constrained copy blocks, and compact proof sections. The new role coverage section adds a strong commercial wedge without turning the page into a long feature catalogue.

## 7. Dopamine / Engagement Score

Score: 8/10

The page now has clearer progression and contrast: rough notes to structured requirements, four role slots to two reviewed workers, internal selection to publish preview, and dispatch action to audit/export handoff.

## 8. Technical Credibility Score

Score: 8/10

The copy now reflects current product truth: corrected credential taxonomy, role counts, separate-worker-only logic, multi-role coverage, CredentialGate, FatigueGuard, SmartRank, publish allocation, manual SMS preview/copy, audit, and CSV exports.

## 9. Pilot-Readiness Score

Score: 9/10

The public page is suitable for major pilot outreach once visual QA and route checks pass. It presents DispatchTalon as commercially useful now while keeping pilot-stage limitations obvious.

## 10. Sections Improved

- Hero: added role coverage and stronger current workflow framing.
- Problem: added role overlap as a specific dispatch risk.
- Role coverage: added the new "A job may need four roles, not four people" commercial wedge.
- Operating modes: added multi-role coverage and publish allocation to labour-only and plant + labour workflows.

- The dispatcher-control boundary is stated directly and not repeated in every section.

- Legal/trademark clearance remains separate from this content audit.

- Public claims should be re-checked whenever backend capability changes.
