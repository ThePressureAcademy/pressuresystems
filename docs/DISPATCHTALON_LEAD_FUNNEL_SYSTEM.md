# DispatchTalon Lead Funnel System

## 1. Funnel Overview

DispatchTalon lead generation must stay founder-led, evidence-led, and pilot-stage. The goal is not broad self-serve signup. The goal is to find operators with real dispatch pain, qualify whether DispatchTalon can help, run a controlled pilot, collect evidence, and decide whether to continue.

Lead with the commercial wedge:

A job may need four roles, not four people.

Support with the operational truth:

DispatchTalon allocates role coverage, not just names on a roster.

Do not lead with feature inventory, generic AI language, payroll, accounting, compliance, or autonomous dispatch.

## 4. Scoring Model

| Score | Category | Action |
|---:|---|---|
| 22-25 | Priority Design Partner | Founder-led discovery and pilot scope. |
| 18-21 | Strong Pilot Candidate | Book discovery if support burden is acceptable. |
| 14-17 | Testing Partner / Nurture | Use light demo or context-first review. |
| 10-13 | Low Priority | Park unless a specific opportunity exists. |
| <10 | Decline / Park | Do not spend founder time. |

Scoring notes:

- Pain severity measures dispatch pain, not general business interest.
- Decision-maker access matters because pilot setup requires authority.
- Feedback willingness matters because the product is pilot-stage.
- Support burden risk is inverse: high burden should reduce priority even if pain is real.
- Commercial seriousness must be grounded in budget, urgency, and readiness to act.

## 5. Tier Logic

Tier recommendations are internal working model only. Do not publish pricing or treat this document as a quote.

| Tier | Fit | What to prove | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Testing Partner | Small or uncertain fit, narrow feedback value. | Whether the workflow creates clarity at all. | Free support drain. |
| Labour Allocation Pilot | Labour-only operators with credential, fatigue, schedule, or role coverage pain. | Worker/job/SmartRank/publish/audit/export loop. | Plant features distracting the buyer. |
| Founding Partner Pilot | Labour + plant operators with strong workflow pain and feedback willingness. | Plant context, role coverage, audit, exports, support cadence. | Scope creep into fleet, payroll, or compliance. |
| Commercial Pilot | Higher-volume operator needing formal evaluation. | Repeated workflow value, evidence capture, controlled support. | Enterprise expectations before maturity. |
| Managed / On-call Support | High-support pilot where founder/operator support is part of the value. | Whether structured support converts workflow pressure into evidence. | Support load consuming product focus. |

## 6. Weekly Review Cadence

Run a weekly review during active pilots.

| Review item | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Jobs created | Job count and examples. |
| Workers added/imported | Worker count and import friction. |
| SmartRank runs | Count and dispatcher feedback. |
| Role coverage used | Conservative vs suggested headcount examples. |
| Warnings and blocks | CredentialGate, FatigueGuard, schedule, role compatibility. |
| Overrides | Reason quality and frequency. |
| Publish allocation use | Preview and manual publish events. |
| Audit trail usefulness | Whether handover/review improved. |
| Exports | Whether office/admin used CSV handoff. |
| Support load | Time spent, issue categories, unresolved blockers. |
| Decision | Continue, adjust, pause, convert, or exit. |

## 7. Relationship to First-Five Lead Qualification Sheet

The first-five lead qualification sheet is the execution surface for this funnel. This document defines the logic; the sheet records the live lead state.

Use the sheet to:

- Capture the first five targets.
- Score each lead consistently.
- Avoid founder time leakage.
- Keep pricing internal.
- Record next action and owner.
- Preserve product boundaries before discovery.

Do not use the sheet as:

- A public pricing table.
- A CRM replacement.
- A compliance claim.
- A place for private customer or worker data.

## 8. Sales Boundary Script

Use this when a buyer asks whether DispatchTalon automates or approves decisions:

DispatchTalon is decision support. It structures job requirements, worker fit, credentials, fatigue, schedule, role coverage, publish allocation, audit, and exports before dispatch. The dispatcher remains the decision-maker.

Use this when a buyer asks about accounting or payroll:

DispatchTalon is export-first. It provides CSV office handoff data for review. Direct Xero/MYOB integration, payroll calculation, invoice calculation, tax, super, awards, allowances, overtime, and entitlements are not part of the current product claim.

Use this when a buyer asks about SMS:

DispatchTalon currently supports manual SMS preview, copy, and manual publish audit. Direct provider sending is not the current claim.
