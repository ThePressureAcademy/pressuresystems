Founding-partner pilot workflow

LiftIQ is a browser-based dispatcher pilot workflow for crane and lifting operations.

Pressure Systems is developing LiftIQ as a pilot-stage dispatch intelligence layer for operators who need visible reasoning before a dispatcher, planner, or branch lead commits the job. The live pilot workflow now covers worker onboarding, job brief import, timezone-aware scheduling, crane and transport review flags, explainable ranking, override capture, audit-backed proof, and tenant-isolated pilot access.

Current logic path

What LiftIQ does now, in truthful terms.

LiftIQ now supports a browser-based dispatcher pilot workflow: import or create workers, import a job brief or create a job manually, add schedule and requirement details, review crane and transport flags, run explainable SmartRank recommendations, review hard blocks and warnings, confirm allocations, capture override reasons where needed, and keep allocation-level audit proof.

1

Import or create workers

Dispatch teams can onboard workers from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data, then add or tidy records manually when needed.

2

Import a job brief or create a job manually

Dispatchers can paste brief text or import .txt and .md files, then review extracted fields before creating a job.

3

Add schedule, timezone, role, credential, and crane requirements

Jobs carry role, required credentials, timezone-aware windows, crane class, crane model, travel state, counterweight requirement, site access, and setup notes where known.

4

Review crane model, travel state, counterweight, and transport review flags

LiftIQ can calculate a counterweight gap from the selected crane travel state and surface support transport, road-access, NHVR / state notice, and permit review prompts for human confirmation.

5

Run SmartRank

LiftIQ ranks worker options with visible score breakdowns, manual/imported/learned preference signals, CredentialGate hard blocks, and FatigueGuard warnings.

6

Review hard blocks, warnings, schedule conflicts, and preference signals

The dispatcher reviews the visible readiness signals before allocation, including lower-ranked selections, warnings, hard blocks, and schedule conflict cues.

7

Confirm allocation or record override reason

Dispatchers remain the decision-maker and provide an override reason when they select a warning state or lower-ranked worker.

8

Review audit trail and pilot metrics

Confirmed allocations, job brief imports, counterweight transport assessments, warnings, overrides, and audit activity remain visible inside pilot metrics and audit views.

Current pilot truth

Live founding-partner pilot console with bounded workflow scope.

  • Worker import from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data plus manual worker entry.
  • Job Brief Import from pasted text, .txt, and .md with review before job creation.
  • Timezone-aware scheduling, schedule conflict detection, and upcoming job views.
  • Explainable SmartRank recommendations with visible score breakdowns, CredentialGate hard blocks, and FatigueGuard warnings.
  • Crane model, travel-state, counterweight gap, support transport, and road-access review flags.
  • Allocation-level audit events, counterweight assessment events, and pilot metrics within pilot scope.

Boundaries still in place

Dispatcher-led workflow, not autonomous allocation.

  • Dispatcher remains the decision-maker and final confirmer.
  • No payroll, invoicing, quote-builder, maintenance, or FCC-replacement claim.
  • No permit, WHS, legal, or engineering guarantee claim.
  • No route optimisation, permit automation, external calendar sync, OCR/PDF parsing, or inbox ingestion claim.
Decision boundary: LiftIQ does not approve permits, engineer lifts, guarantee compliance, or replace dispatcher judgement. It surfaces structured review points before a dispatch decision is confirmed.
Workflow fit

LiftIQ sits beside dispatch, workforce, and planning workflows.

It is not being framed as an isolated scoring toy. The pilot workflow connects worker data, job context, visible checks, allocation confirmation, and audit proof while keeping maturity claims disciplined.

Dispatch Import workers, create jobs, rank crew-fit options, review warnings, and confirm allocations with visible reasoning support.
Workforce Credential visibility, fatigue exposure, repeated over-allocation awareness, and preference signals that remain explainable.
Audit Append-only allocation-level audit events and pilot metrics exist inside the pilot workflow.
Future integrations External systems, route workflows, permit workflows, OCR/PDF parsing, inbox ingestion, and calendar sync remain future questions, not current support.
Truth boundary: LiftIQ does not claim autonomous dispatch, WHS/legal compliance guarantees, payroll logic, invoice automation, route optimisation, permit automation, OCR/PDF parsing, inbox ingestion, external calendar sync, or live external integrations on this page. Those remain later architecture questions only if pilot evidence and client system access justify them.
