Current pilot-backed modules

LiftIQ's module story is strongest when current pilot-backed capability stays separate from future ideas.

Pressure Systems keeps the module states separated on purpose. The current modules below are backed by the founding-partner pilot workflow. Future or not-yet layers stay explicitly outside the headline until there is real product surface and justified integration depth.

Module Status Current truth Commercial boundary
Worker Import Pilot-backed Workers can be imported from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data, then completed or corrected manually inside the workflow. Public as worker onboarding support. Not a full HR, payroll, or licensing system.
Job Brief Import Pilot-backed Job briefs can be imported from pasted text, .txt, and .md, with extracted fields reviewed before job creation. Public as structured job intake. Not OCR, PDF parsing, inbox ingestion, or autonomous job creation.
SmartRank Pilot-backed Explainable worker recommendations show score breakdowns, recommended workers, warnings, hard blocks, and preference contributions. Public as decision support. Not autonomous dispatch or final-decision replacement.
CredentialGate Pilot-backed Required credentials are checked before allocation and missing mandatory requirements stay visibly hard-blocked. Public as pre-allocation credential checks. Not automated legal or regulatory assurance.
FatigueGuard Pilot-backed Fatigue warnings and hard blocks are applied before allocation using recorded fatigue and rest inputs. Public as fatigue visibility and warning logic. Not a WHS guarantee.
Dispatch Calendar / Schedule Pilot-backed Jobs can be scheduled with timezone-aware windows and shown in the dispatch schedule. Public as pilot scheduling. Not external calendar sync.
Schedule Conflict Detection Pilot-backed Schedule conflicts are surfaced before allocation so dispatcher review can happen earlier. Public as readiness warning logic. Not an automated dispatch decision.
Task Preferences Pilot-backed Task-specific worker preference ratings can influence SmartRank visibly. Public as explainable weighting. Hard blocks still override preference signals.
Adaptive Learned Preferences Pilot-backed Dispatcher-confirmed allocations can contribute learned preference signals over time. Public as adaptive preference support. Not black-box automation.
Safe Worker Removal Pilot-backed Workers can be archived without destroying historical jobs, allocations, or audit context. Public as data-preserving removal. Not a full HR offboarding workflow.
Multi-Company Pilot Access Pilot-backed Tenant-isolated pilot workspaces support company-specific login, data separation, access status, and test-period control. Public as tenant-isolated pilot access. Not public self-serve signup or company switching.
Crane Counterweight + Transport Planning Pilot-backed Exact crane model and travel-state selection can calculate counterweight gaps and flag support transport, road-access, NHVR / state notice, and permit review points. Public as planning support and review prompts. Not permit approval, route optimisation, or lift engineering.
AuditIQ Pilot-backed Allocations, job brief imports, counterweight transport assessments, and transport requirement creation write audit events inside pilot scope. Public as allocation-level audit trail. Not external regulatory reporting.
Pilot Metrics Pilot-backed Pilot metrics summarize imports, warnings, allocations, overrides, audit activity, and related workflow activity. Public as pilot proof view. Not advanced analytics or legal reporting.
Future / not-yet modules Separated Full ERP replacement, payroll, invoicing, quote builder, permit automation, route optimisation, GPS / telematics, email inbox ingestion, OCR / PDF parsing, and external calendar sync are not current pilot modules. Keep these out of current capability claims unless a later build actually ships them.
Most defensible now

Structured review before dispatch

Visible SmartRank scoring, warning states, hard blocks, schedule conflicts, crane requirements, and transport review prompts make the decision reviewable under pressure.

Most sensitive now

Credential, fatigue, and road-access language

LiftIQ can show pre-allocation checks and review prompts clearly. It cannot claim permit approval, WHS guarantees, lift engineering confirmation, or automatic incident prevention.

Most important later

Future layers stay separated

The module story stays credible by separating current pilot-backed surfaces from later ERP, finance, route, permit, OCR, inbox, calendar, or telematics ideas.

Current pilot-backed capability

  • Live founding-partner pilot console with tenant-isolated pilot access.
  • Worker import, Job Brief Import, scheduled jobs, schedule conflict detection, credentials, fatigue records, SmartRank, and allocation confirmation.
  • Crane model, travel-state, counterweight gap, support transport, and road-access review flags.
  • Allocation-level audit trail, counterweight assessment events, pilot metrics, and manual/imported/learned preference signals.

Future or not promoted now

  • No payroll, invoicing, quote builder, maintenance, billing, or FCC-replacement claim.
  • No autonomous dispatch, black-box allocation, permit automation, route optimisation, or lift engineering claim.
  • No live Xero, MYOB, external calendar, GPS / telematics, email inbox ingestion, OCR, or PDF parsing claim.
  • No public self-serve signup or company switcher claim.
Commercial use

Use the current pilot-backed layer to qualify fit. Keep the future layer out of the headline.

The strongest commercial movement still starts with a LiftIQ Discovery Call or a context-first founder review, not a long list of future module names.
