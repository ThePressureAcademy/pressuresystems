Current pilot-backed modules

LiftIQ's module story is strongest when current pilot-backed capability stays separate from future ideas.

Pressure Systems keeps the module states separated on purpose. The modules below are backed by the current founding-partner pilot workflow. Future layers stay explicitly outside the headline until there is real product surface and justified integration depth.

Module Status Current truth Commercial boundary
SmartRank Demonstrated Explainable crew-fit ranking with visible score breakdowns, recommended workers, warnings, and hard blocks. Safe to present as explainable recommendation logic. Unsafe to present as autonomous dispatch.
FatigueGuard Demonstrated Fatigue warnings and hard blocks are applied before allocation using recorded fatigue and rest inputs. Safe to present as visible fatigue checks, not a WHS guarantee.
CredentialGate Demonstrated Required credentials are checked before allocation and missing mandatory requirements stay visibly hard-blocked. Safe to present as pre-allocation credential checks, not automated compliance software.
Worker Import Demonstrated Workers can be imported from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data, then completed or corrected manually inside the workflow. Safe as founding-partner onboarding. Not a full HR or payroll integration.
Allocation Workflow Demonstrated Dispatchers confirm allocations, warning or lower-ranked selections require override reasons, and backend 422 rejections stay visible. Safe as dispatcher-led confirmation logic. Not a dispatch replacement claim.
AuditIQ Demonstrated Confirmed allocations create append-only audit events inside the pilot workflow. Safe to present as allocation-level audit trail inside pilot scope, not external regulatory reporting.
Preference Signals Demonstrated Manual, imported, and learned worker preference signals are visible in SmartRank and contribute explainably. Safe as transparent weighting. Hard blocks still override preference signals.
Pilot Metrics Demonstrated Screenshot-friendly pilot metrics summarize jobs, allocations, blocks, warnings, overrides, and audit activity. Safe as operational proof view. Not advanced analytics or compliance reporting.
External System Alignment Specified Xero, MYOB, dispatch-system access, and broader write-back remain future architecture questions only. Do not imply live integrations or finance workflow support.
Autonomous Dispatch Deferred There is no autonomous allocator and no final-decision replacement path in the current product. Do not market LiftIQ as self-directing dispatch software.
Most defensible now

Explainable score breakdowns

Visible SmartRank scoring, warning states, hard blocks, and preference contributions make the recommendation reviewable under pressure.

Most sensitive now

Credential and fatigue language

LiftIQ can show pre-allocation checks clearly. It cannot claim compliance approval, WHS guarantees, or automatic incident prevention.

Most important later

Operational system alignment

The module story becomes stronger only if later partner discovery justifies deeper source-system access and finance alignment work.

Current pilot-backed capability

  • Live founding-partner pilot console.
  • Worker import, credentials, fatigue records, jobs, SmartRank, and allocation confirmation.
  • Append-only allocation-level audit trail and pilot metrics.
  • Manual, imported, and learned preference signals that remain explainable.

Future or not promoted now

  • No payroll, invoicing, or FCC-replacement claim.
  • No autonomous dispatch or black-box allocation claim.
  • No live Xero or MYOB integration claim.
  • No chat assistant or predictive layer promoted as live capability.
Commercial use

Use the current pilot-backed layer to qualify fit. Keep the future layer out of the headline.

The strongest commercial movement still starts with a LiftIQ Discovery Call or a context-first founder review, not a long list of future module names.
