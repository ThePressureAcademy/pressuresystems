Why this matters

Most allocation risk is not dramatic. It is cumulative.

Late call-outs. Repeated crew assumptions. Ticket checks that happen too late. Fatigue that stays fragmented across the day. Each issue looks manageable on its own. Together they create scramble, delay, and avoidable decision noise unless the workflow stays visible.

Dispatch blind spot

The board looks stable until one change exposes three weak assumptions.

Manual crew-fit logic often depends on memory, not a visible reasoning layer. That is manageable until urgency strips the buffer out of the day.

Credential risk

Required tickets get discovered under pressure instead of before the call.

LiftIQ makes missing mandatory requirements obvious before allocation instead of burying them in notes, memory, or a hurried phone chain.

Fatigue visibility

Hours and rest history stay fragmented when the day starts to move fast.

Fatigue exposure is operational, not theoretical. LiftIQ records the signal, surfaces warnings and hard blocks, and keeps the decision visible to the dispatcher.

What LiftIQ is

A browser-based dispatcher pilot console for allocation-heavy crane and lifting workflows.

LiftIQ is a browser-based dispatcher pilot console for allocation-heavy crane and lifting workflows. Teams can import workers, import job briefs, schedule jobs, check credentials and fatigue, review crane and transport requirements, run SmartRank recommendations, confirm allocations, and review audit-backed pilot metrics.

Pilot-backed

Job intake

Import job briefs from pasted text, .txt, or .md. Review extracted fields before creating a job, then edit date, time, timezone, site, crew, credentials, and notes before confirmation.

Pilot-backed

Worker onboarding

Import workers from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data, track credentials, fatigue records, and task-specific preferences, and remove or archive workers without destroying audit history.

Pilot-backed

Scheduled dispatch

Plan jobs weeks ahead, store timezone-aware job windows, detect schedule conflicts before allocation, and show upcoming jobs in the dispatch schedule.

Pilot-backed

SmartRank

Run explainable worker recommendations with CredentialGate hard blocks, FatigueGuard warnings and hard blocks, and override reasons for warning or lower-ranked selections.

Pilot-backed

Crane and transport planning

Select exact crane model and travel state, calculate counterweight gaps, flag support transport requirements, and show road-access / NHVR review prompts without permit approval claims.

Pilot-backed

Audit and proof

Track allocation-level audit events, job brief import events, counterweight transport assessment events, and pilot metrics for imports, warnings, allocations, overrides, and audit activity.

Pilot-backed

Multi-company pilot access

Support tenant-isolated pilot workspaces, company-specific login and data separation, pilot access status, and test-period support without a company switcher or public self-serve signup.

How it fits

LiftIQ supports dispatch decisions without stepping around the dispatcher.

The current product direction is deliberate: browser-based pilot workflow, structured job intake, explainable recommendation before any broader automation claim, and explicit human review before any allocation is confirmed.

1

Import workers and job briefs

Dispatch teams can onboard workers from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data, then import job briefs from pasted text, .txt, or .md for human review before job creation.

2

Add schedule, credentials, fatigue, and crane requirements

Timezone-aware job windows, required credentials, fatigue records, crane model, travel state, counterweight requirement, site access, and setup notes can be reviewed before ranking.

3

Review readiness warnings and run SmartRank

LiftIQ surfaces schedule conflicts, CredentialGate hard blocks, FatigueGuard warnings, counterweight transport review flags, and explainable SmartRank recommendations before allocation.

4

Confirm allocation with visible reasoning

Dispatchers remain the decision-maker, capture an override reason where warning or lower-ranked selections require it, and keep hard blocks visible.

5

Keep audit proof and learn from confirmed patterns

Confirmed allocations, job brief imports, and counterweight transport assessments write audit events, update pilot metrics, and let learned preference signals influence future rankings without overriding hard blocks.
