Why this matters

Most allocation risk is not dramatic. It is cumulative.

Late call-outs. Repeated crew assumptions. Ticket checks that happen too late. Fatigue that stays fragmented across the day. Each issue looks manageable on its own. Together they create scramble, delay, and avoidable decision noise unless the workflow stays visible.

Dispatch blind spot

The board looks stable until one change exposes three weak assumptions.

Manual crew-fit logic often depends on memory, not a visible reasoning layer. That is manageable until urgency strips the buffer out of the day.

Credential risk

Required tickets get discovered under pressure instead of before the call.

LiftIQ makes missing mandatory requirements obvious before allocation instead of burying them in notes, memory, or a hurried phone chain.

Fatigue visibility

Hours and rest history stay fragmented when the day starts to move fast.

Fatigue exposure is operational, not theoretical. LiftIQ records the signal, surfaces warnings and hard blocks, and keeps the decision visible to the dispatcher.

How it fits

LiftIQ supports dispatch decisions without stepping around the dispatcher.

The current product direction is deliberate: browser-based pilot workflow, explainable recommendation before any broader automation claim, and explicit human review before any allocation is confirmed.

1

Import or create workers

Dispatch teams can onboard workers from CSV or pasted spreadsheet data, then fill gaps with manual worker entry where needed.

2

Record credentials and fatigue

Worker credentials and fatigue records become part of the live pilot workflow before the job is ranked or confirmed.

3

Create jobs and run SmartRank

LiftIQ ranks crew-fit options, shows visible score breakdowns, and surfaces CredentialGate hard blocks plus FatigueGuard warnings before allocation.

4

Confirm allocation with visible reasoning

Dispatchers remain the decision-maker, capture an override reason where warning or lower-ranked selections require it, and keep backend hard-blocks visible.

5

Keep audit proof and learn from approved patterns

Confirmed allocations write append-only audit events, update pilot metrics, and let learned preference signals influence future rankings without overriding hard blocks.
