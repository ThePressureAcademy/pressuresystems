Who this is for

Serious operators with real dispatch complexity.

  • Testing Partners who want a short, bounded workflow reality check.
  • Founding Partners with operational complexity worth validating in a controlled pilot.
  • Commercial Pilot candidates willing to review fit, access, boundaries, and human decision control before broad feature claims.
What a design partner gets

Structured working sessions, not vague interest.

A bounded briefing, product walkthrough, pilot suitability review, and clear current-state boundaries so the conversation stays useful.

What Pressure Systems gets

Real workflow truth from operators who actually run the pressure.

Access to planning logic, edge cases, data realities, site and transport review issues, system constraints, and operational objections that should shape the next build phase.

What this is not

Not a procurement-ready rollout.

No claims of production-readiness, autonomous dispatch, permit decisions, lift engineering, live integrations, or measured ROI are required or appropriate at this stage.

Proof sequence: public LiftIQ pages establish the category, current boundaries, and the right next conversation. Pressure Systems shares the fictional sample preview only after a serious fit discussion exists. It is a stage-two proof asset, not a cold lead magnet.
First engagement shape

A credible first engagement has clear boundaries.

LiftIQ should enter through a real operational problem, a private briefing, and a pilot suitability conversation. That keeps the design-partner model commercially serious and protects against overselling.

1

Executive briefing

Pressure Systems walks through the product position, current maturity, demonstrated logic, and why LiftIQ is being developed as a decision-support overlay rather than a dispatch replacement.

2

Pilot suitability review

The operator and Pressure Systems check whether the data, workflow pain, and internal sponsorship are strong enough to justify a controlled pilot conversation.

3

Phase-1 operational focus

Worker import, job brief import, scheduled dispatch, credential confidence, fatigue visibility, explainable recommendation behaviour, audit, and crane/counterweight review flags remain the core scope. No finance integration is assumed.

4

Phase-2 pathway only if justified

If the pilot proves useful and the partner wants deeper commercial visibility, later architecture discovery may include integrations, routing, permit workflow, or finance-system questions. Those remain planned, access-dependent work only.

Credible to say now

  • LiftIQ is a founding-partner pilot with a browser-based dispatcher workflow.
  • LiftIQ supports worker import, job brief import, scheduled jobs, SmartRank, hard blocks, warnings, audit, crane/counterweight review flags, and tenant-isolated pilot access inside pilot scope.
  • LiftIQ is being positioned for tightly controlled testing partner, founding partner, and commercial pilot conversations.
  • Founding-partner pilot pricing is discussed after fit check and workflow review.

Do not claim

  • That LiftIQ is production-ready or enterprise-ready.
  • That LiftIQ is live-integrated with dispatch software, Xero, MYOB, external calendars, GPS / telematics, email inboxes, OCR, or PDF parsing.
  • That LiftIQ approves permits, engineers lifts, guarantees compliance, or functions as a regulatory reporting platform.
  • That LiftIQ has proven ROI or live customer outcomes.
